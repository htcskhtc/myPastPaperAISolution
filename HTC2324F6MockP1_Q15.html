Question 15

In a library box, there are 5 books with red covers and 4 books with black covers. From the box, 2 books are chosen randomly at the same time.

  1. (a) Find the probability that both chosen books have red covers. (2 marks)
  2. (b) Paul has a school bag containing 8 books with red covers. He chooses 2 books from the library box and puts them into his school bag. Then, 3 books are chosen randomly from his school bag at the same time. Find the probability that all 3 chosen books have the same cover color. (2 marks)
Solution
Part (a)

Total number of books in the box = 5+4=9.

We choose 2 books from the box.

The probability that both books are red is:

P(2 Red)=C25C29 =1036 =518
Concept Note: Since the books are chosen "at the same time", we use Combinations (Crn). Alternatively, you could use sequential probability without replacement: 59Ã—48=518.
Part (b)

Initial State: The bag contains 8 Red books.

Process: 2 books are transferred from the box to the bag. Then, 3 books are chosen from the bag.

Condition: All 3 books chosen from the bag must have the "same cover color".

Let's analyze the colors. The bag starts with only Red books. We add at most 2 Black books (if we drew 2 Black books from the box). Therefore, the bag contains at most 2 Black books.

To choose 3 books of the same color, they must be Red. It is impossible to choose 3 Black books because there are at most 2.

We consider 3 mutually exclusive cases based on the books transferred from the box:

Case 1: 2 Red books transferred

  • Probability of transfer (from part a): 518
  • Bag now contains: 8+2=10 Red, 0 Black.
  • Probability of choosing 3 Red from bag: C310C310=1
  • P(Case 1)=518Ã—1=518

Case 2: 1 Red and 1 Black book transferred

  • Probability of transfer: C15Ã—C14C29=5Ã—436=2036=59
  • Bag now contains: 8+1=9 Red, 1 Black. Total 10.
  • Probability of choosing 3 Red from bag: C39C310=84120=710
  • P(Case 2)=59Ã—710=3590

Case 3: 2 Black books transferred

  • Probability of transfer: C24C29=636=16
  • Bag now contains: 8 Red, 2 Black. Total 10.
  • Probability of choosing 3 Red from bag: C38C310=56120=715
  • P(Case 3)=16Ã—715=790

Total Probability:

P(All 3 Red)=518+3590+790 =2590+3590+790 =6790
Common Trap: Students often forget to check if it is possible to draw 3 Black books. Always check the total count of each color available. Since there are max 2 Black books in the bag, P(3 Black) is 0.

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes
  • Mutually Exclusive Events: When different scenarios cannot happen at the same time (e.g., transferring 2 Red vs. transferring 1 Red 1 Black), calculate the probability for each case and sum them up.
  • Total Probability: Always double-check fractions when summing. Converting to a common denominator (like 90 here) early prevents arithmetic errors.
Strategy
  • Case Analysis: Break the problem down based on the "first stage" (what happens in the box). This determines the contents of the "second stage" (the bag).
  • Feasibility Check: Before calculating, ask "Is it possible to get 3 Black books?". Recognizing impossible events (P=0) saves time.
Required Skill
  • Combinations (nCr): Used for selecting items without order.
  • Conditional Probability: Adjusting the second probability based on the outcome of the first event.
