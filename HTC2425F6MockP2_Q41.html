  1. Denote the centroid, the in-centre and the circumcentre of △XYZ by G, I and J respectively. If ∠XYZ=36∘ and ∠XZY=54∘, which of the following are true?
    1. X is the orthocentre of △XYZ.
    2. J is the mid-point of YZ.
    3. G, I and J are collinear.
    1. I and II only
    2. I and III only
    3. II and III only
    4. I, II and III

Solution

Step 1: Analyze the triangle type
Consider △XYZ. We are given: ∠XYZ=36∘ ∠XZY=54∘ The sum of angles in a triangle is 180∘. Therefore: ∠YXZ=180∘−36∘−54∘=90∘ So, △XYZ is a right-angled triangle with the right angle at X.
Concept Note: Triangle Properties Identifying that the triangle is right-angled is the key to solving this problem. The positions of the Orthocentre and Circumcentre are special for right-angled triangles.

∴ Statement I is True.
Step 3: Analyze Statement II (J is the mid-point of YZ)

∴ Statement II is True.
Step 4: Analyze Statement III (G, I and J are collinear)

∴ Statement III is False.
Common Trap Students often remember that the Orthocentre, Centroid, and Circumcentre are collinear (Euler Line). However, the In-centre generally does not lie on the Euler line unless the triangle is isosceles.
Conclusion
Statements I and II are true. Statement III is false.
The correct option is A.

Notes / Strategies

Key Properties for Right-Angled Triangles
  • Orthocentre: Located at the vertex of the right angle.
  • Circumcentre: Located at the mid-point of the hypotenuse.
  • Centroid: Located on the median, dividing it in a 2:1 ratio.
  • Euler Line: Connects Orthocentre, Centroid, and Circumcentre.
Notes
  • Memorize that for a right-angled △, the orthocentre is the 90∘ vertex.
  • Memorize that the circumcentre is the mid-point of the hypotenuse.
  • The in-centre lies on angle bisectors; it does not usually lie on the median.
Strategy
  1. Calculate the third angle to identify the type of triangle (90∘ implies right-angled).
  2. Recall the definitions and locations of centers for special triangles.
  3. To check collinearity involving the in-centre, calculate the angles made by the relevant lines (angle bisector vs median).
Required Skill
  • Knowledge of Triangle Centers (G,I,J,O).
  • Angle chasing in triangles.
  • Properties of isosceles triangles formed by the median to the hypotenuse.
