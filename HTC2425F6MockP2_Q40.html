Method: Geometric Construction (Using Cross-section VMC)

As illustrated in the solution figure above, the problem can be reduced to the 2D cross-section plane containing the altitude of the tetrahedron and the line IC.

1. Define Dimensions
Let the side length of the regular tetrahedron be 2a.
The faces are equilateral triangles. M is the mid-point of AB.

2. Analyze Cross-section VMC
VM and CM are altitudes of the equilateral faces VAB and ABC respectively.
VM=CM=2asin60=3a VC=2a.
Thus, VMC is an isosceles triangle with sides (3a,3a,2a).

Concept Notes & Common Traps

1. Reducing 3D to 2D:
The key to solving regular tetrahedron problems is often finding the correct 2D cross-section. Here, the plane passing through the edge VC and the midpoint M of the opposite side (AB) contains all the necessary points (V,M,C,I).

2. Similar Triangles for Heights:
If a point I lies on a line segment VM connected to the apex, its height from the base is strictly proportional to its position on the segment. Height(I)Height(V)=IMVM This is often faster than calculating Cartesian coordinates.

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes
  • Isosceles Cross-Section: The triangle formed by an edge (VC) and the altitude of the base (CM) and face (VM) is isosceles.
  • Cosine Rule Application: Used to find the angle in the cross-section to locate point I.
Strategy
  1. Visualize the Cross-Section: Refer to Figure 2. Isolate VMC.
  2. Use Ratios: Establish the position of I using the ratio 1:3.
  3. Scale the Height: Calculate the height of I as 1/3 of the total height.
  4. Definition of Sine: sin=Opposite (Height)/Hypotenuse (IC).
