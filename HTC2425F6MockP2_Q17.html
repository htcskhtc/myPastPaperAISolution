Question 17

  1. In the figure, ABCD is a square. Let E be a point lying on BC such that BE:EC=2:3. Let F be a point lying on CD such that CF:FD=2:3. AE and BF intersect at the point G. If the area of BEG is 8 cm2, then the area of AGF is
    1. 87 cm2.
    2. 95 cm2.
    3. 100 cm2.
    4. 145 cm2.

Solution (Geometric Approach)

Let the side length of the square ABCD be s.
Given BE:EC=2:3, we have BE=25s.
Given CF:FD=2:3, we have CF=25s.

Step 1: Proving Congruence & Perpendicularity (Figure 2)

Consider ABE and BCF:

  • AB=BC=s (Sides of square)
  • ABE=BCF=90 (Angles of square)
  • BE=CF=25s (Given ratio)

ABEBCF (SAS).

From congruence, BAE=CBF.
Let BAE=. Then CBF=.
In ABG, ABG=90CBF=90.
Sum of angles in ABG:
AGB=180(BAG+ABG)=180(+90)=90.
Conclusion: AEBF.

Step 2: Using Similarity to Find Total Areas

Consider BEG and ABE. Both are right-angled triangles (at G and B respectively) and share GBE.

BEGABE (AA).

Calculate the ratio of sides:

  • Hypotenuse of BEG is BE=25s=0.4s.
  • Hypotenuse of ABE is AE=AB2+BE2=s2+(0.4s)2=1.16s2=295s.

Area Ratio = (BEAE)2=(0.4s295s)2=(229)2=429.

Given Area(BEG) = 8.

Area(BEG)Area(ABE)=429Area(ABE)=8294=58.

Step 3: Calculating Final Area (Figure 3)

We need to find the Area of AGF.

First, find the Area of ABG:

Area(ABG) = Area(ABE) - Area(BEG) = 588=50.

Next, find the Area of ABF. Taking AB=s as the base, the height is the perpendicular distance from F to AB, which is s (since F lies on CD).

Note: We first find s2. Area(ABE) = 12s0.4s=0.2s2.
0.2s2=58s2=290.

Area(ABF) = 12ss=12s2=12(290)=145.

Finally, subtract to find AGF:

Area(AGF) = Area(ABF) - Area(ABG)

Area(AGF) = 14550=95 cm2.

The answer is (B).

Notes & Strategy

Note on Figure 2 & 3: These figures illustrate the "Rotation and Decomposition" method. By rotating ABG or recognizing the congruence, we establish the perpendicular framework. Then, decomposing ABF allows us to calculate the target area by simple subtraction.

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes
  • Congruent Triangles in Squares: A line from a vertex to the opposite side with ratio 2:3 (like AE and BF) often creates congruent triangles (ABEBCF) and perpendicular intersections.
  • Area Ratio of Similar Triangles: The ratio of areas is the square of the ratio of corresponding sides (A1A2=(l1l2)2).
Strategy
  • Geometric Decomposition: Instead of finding coordinates of G, use the property that BEGABE. Solve for the total area of ABE, then find the area of the "middle" triangle ABG by subtraction.
  • Target Area by Subtraction: Area(AGF)=Area(ABF)Area(ABG).
Required Skill
  • Identifying similar and congruent triangles.
  • Calculating area ratios.
  • decomposing complex areas into simpler triangles.
