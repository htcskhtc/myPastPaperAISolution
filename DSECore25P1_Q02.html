Question 2 (DSE25PI_Q02)

2.    Simplify 13d426d+5. (3 marks)

Solution

Concept Note: Finding the Common Denominator To subtract algebraic fractions, you must find a common denominator. Here, the denominators are (3d4) and (6d+5). Since they share no common factors, the Least Common Multiple (LCM) is simply their product: (3d4)(6d+5).
13d426d+5=1(6d+5)(3d4)(6d+5)2(3d4)(3d4)(6d+5)=(6d+5)2(3d4)(3d4)(6d+5)
Common Trap: Negative Sign Distribution When expanding 2(3d4), be extremely careful with the signs.
Many students write 6d8, which is incorrect.
The correct expansion is 23d=6d and 24=+8.
=6d+5(6d8)(3d4)(6d+5)=6d+56d+8(3d4)(6d+5)=13(3d4)(6d+5).
13(3d4)(6d+5) (Valid for d43 and d56.)
Additional Notes
  • Strategies: Always convert to a single fraction by finding the LCM of the denominators.
  • Key Skill: Expansion of algebraic expressions involving negative signs.
  • Formatting: It is standard practice in HKDSE to leave the denominator in factored form (e.g., (3d4)(6d+5)) rather than expanding it, unless the question explicitly asks to "expand" or "express as a polynomial".

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes
  • Sign Errors: The most common mistake in this type of question is sign errors during the expansion of the numerator (e.g., A(BC)ABAC).
  • Denominator: Do not expand the denominator in the final answer unless requested. Factored form is preferred.
Strategy
  1. Identify the LCM of the denominators.
  2. Multiply the numerator and denominator of each fraction by the necessary factor to achieve the common denominator.
  3. Combine the numerators over the single denominator.
  4. Expand and simplify the numerator (combine like terms).
Required Skill
  • Rational Function Operations (Subtraction).
  • Algebraic Expansion and Simplification.
