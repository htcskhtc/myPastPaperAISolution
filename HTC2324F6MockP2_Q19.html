19. It is given that ABCD is a rhombus. Denote the point of intersection of AC and BD by E. Which of the following must be true?

  1. ABECDE
  2. ABD is an equilateral triangle.
  3. AEBD=ACBE
  1. I only
  2. II only
  3. I and III only
  4. II and III only

Solution

Reference Solution Image:

Detailed Analysis & Explanation:

Step 1: Recall Properties of a Rhombus
For any rhombus ABCD with diagonals intersecting at E:

  • All four sides are equal: AB=BC=CD=DA.
  • Diagonals bisect each other: AE=CE and BE=DE.
  • Diagonals are perpendicular: ACBD (i.e., AEB=90).

Step 2: Analyze Statement I
Consider ABE and CDE:

  • AB=CD (Sides of a rhombus)
  • AE=CE (Diagonals of a parallelogram/rhombus bisect each other)
  • BE=DE (Diagonals of a parallelogram/rhombus bisect each other)
ABECDE (SSS).
Alternatively, since the rhombus has rotational symmetry of order 2 about E, ABE maps directly onto CDE.
Statement I must be true.

Step 3: Analyze Statement II
For ABD to be equilateral, we need AB=AD=BD.
We know AB=AD (properties of a rhombus), so ABD is always an isosceles triangle.
However, BD is a diagonal. The length of the diagonal BD depends on the interior angles of the rhombus. BD=AB only if BAD=60. Since the question does not specify the angle, this is not necessarily true.
Statement II is NOT necessarily true.

Step 4: Analyze Statement III
We examine the equation: AEBD=ACBE.
Since diagonals bisect each other:

  • AC=2AEAE=12AC
  • BD=2BEBE=12BD
Substitute AE=12AC into the Left Hand Side (LHS):
LHS =(12AC)BD=12ACBD
Substitute BE=12BD into the Right Hand Side (RHS):
RHS =AC(12BD)=12ACBD
Since LHS = RHS, the equation holds.
Statement III must be true.

Conclusion: Statements I and III are true.

Answer: C

Concept Notes & Strategy

1. Rhombus Definition: A rhombus is a parallelogram with four equal sides.

2. Key Properties to Memorize:

  • Diagonals bisect each other at right angles (90).
  • Diagonals bisect the interior angles.

3. Common Trap: Students often confuse a rhombus with a square. While a square is a rhombus, a generic rhombus does not have 90 interior angles, nor are its diagonals equal in length (ACBD usually).

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes (Formulas & Concepts)
  • Rhombus Properties:
    • Sides: AB=BC=CD=DA
    • Diagonals: Bisect each other at 90 (ACBD).
    • Angles: Opposite angles are equal; diagonals bisect vertex angles.
  • Congruence (SSS): If three sides of one triangle are equal to three sides of another, they are congruent.
Strategy
  • "Must be true" Questions: To prove a statement is false, try to find a single counter-example (e.g., imagine a very thin or very wide rhombus to test Statement II).
  • Algebraic Verification: For statements like III involving lengths, substitute known relationships (e.g., midpoint formulas) to check for identity.
