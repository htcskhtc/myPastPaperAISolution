Question 8

Let O be the origin. The position vectors of A and B are ij+3k and 2jk respectively. Let C be a point on AB such that OCAB. Denote the orthocentre of OAB by H.

  1. By considering the ratio of AC to CB, or otherwise, find OC. (3 marks)
    1. Find OH.
    2. Someone claims that H is outside OAB. Do you agree? Explain your answer.
    (3 marks)
  3. Let be the plane which contains OAB.
    1. Find a non-zero vector which is perpendicular to .
    2. Let G be the centre of the circle passing through C, A and H. Denote the projection of point D(2,8,43) on by P. Describe the geometric relationship between O, P and G. Explain your answer.
    (6 marks)

Solution

Part (a): Find OC

Let the position vectors be:

OA=ij+3k OB=2jk

Since C lies on AB, we can express OC using a parameter t (Section Formula):

OC=(1t)OA+tOB OC=(1t)(ij+3k)+t(2jk) OC=(1t)i+(1t+2t)j+(33tt)k OC=(1t)i+(1t)j+(34t)k

Since OCAB, the scalar product OCAB=0.

First, find AB:

AB=OBOA=(2jk)(ij+3k)=ij4k

Now, apply the perpendicularity condition using the coefficients of i,j,k:

(1t)(1)+(1t)(1)+(34t)(4)=0 1+t+1+t12+16t=0 18t12=018t=12t=23

Substitute t=23 back into OC:

OC=(123)i+(123)j+(34(23))k OC=13i53j+13k=13(i5j+k)
Concept Note: Section Formula & Dot Product

When a point C lies on a line segment AB, any vector OC can be written as a linear combination of OA and OB. The condition OCAB implies the dot product of their vector forms is zero.

Part (b)(i): Find OH

In OAB, OC is the altitude from O to AB because OCAB. Since H is the orthocentre, H must lie on the line containing the altitude OC.

Therefore, OH is parallel to OC. Let OH=kOC for some scalar k.

OH=k(13i53j+13k)

By definition of the orthocentre, the line segment from a vertex to H is perpendicular to the opposite side. Thus, AHOB.

AHOB=0 (OHOA)OB=0

Substitute the vectors:

[(k3i5k3j+k3k)(ij+3k)](2jk)=0 [(k31)i+(5k3+1)j+(k33)k](2jk)=0 0(k31)2(5k3+1)1(k33)=0 10k32k3+3=0 9k3+1=03k=1k=13

Thus,

OH=13OC=13(13i53j+13k)=19i+59j19k
Common Trap: Location of Orthocentre

Students often assume H lies "inside" the triangle. The key is that H lies on the lines containing the altitudes. Using the vector relationship OH=kOC simplifies the problem significantly compared to solving simultaneous equations.

Part (b)(ii): Is H outside OAB?

We found that OH=13OC.

Since the scalar multiple is negative (k=1/3), OH and OC are in opposite directions. This means O lies between H and C. Consequently, H lies on the extension of the altitude CO outside the triangle.

Alternatively, checking the angle AOB:

OAOB=(1)(0)+(1)(2)+(3)(1)=0+23=1

Since OAOB<0, cosAOB<0, which implies AOB is obtuse (>90).

Conclusion: Yes, I agree. Since OAB is an obtuse-angled triangle, its orthocentre H lies outside the triangle.

A non-zero vector perpendicular to is 7i+j2k.

Part (c)(ii): Geometric relationship between O,P,G

1. Find OD and PD:

The position vector of D is OD=2i8j+43k.

Since O lies on the plane and P is the projection of D onto , the vector PD is the vector component of OD parallel to the normal vector n. We can find PD by calculating the projection of OD onto n.

PD=projnOD=(ODn|n|2)n

First, calculate the dot product and magnitude squared:

ODn=(2)(7)+(8)(1)+(43)(2)=14886=108 |n|2=72+12+(2)2=49+1+4=54

Now, calculate PD:

PD=10854n=2(7i+j2k)=14i2j+4k

2. Find OP:

By vector addition, OD=OP+PD. Therefore:

OP=ODPD OP=(2i8j+43k)(14i2j+4k) OP=(2+14)i+(8+2)j+(434)k OP=12i6j+39k

3. Find OG:

G is the centre of the circle passing through C,A,H.

Consider the geometry: C lies on AB, and OC is the altitude perpendicular to AB. Thus OCA=90. Since HCA=90, the segment AH must be the diameter of the circle passing through C,A,H.

Therefore, G is the midpoint of AH.

OG=OA+OH2 OG=12[(ij+3k)+(19i+59j19k)] OG=12(89i49j+269k)=49i29j+139k

We can write this as OG=19(4i2j+13k).

4. Compare OG and OP:

OP=12i6j+39k=3(4i2j+13k) OG=19(4i2j+13k)

It is clear that:

OP=27OG

Conclusion: Since OP is a scalar multiple of OG, the points O, G, and P are collinear.

(Specifically, P lies on the line passing through O and G.)

Note in Latex (Summary)

Concept Notes:

  • For (a): Dot product is zero for perpendicular vectors.
  • For (b): The orthocentre lies on the altitudes. If OAOB<0, the angle at O is obtuse, placing H "behind" O.
  • For (c): The circle passing through vertices of a right-angled triangle (ACH) has the hypotenuse (AH) as diameter.

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes
  • Perpendicular Vectors: ab=0.
  • Plane Normal: n=ab.
  • Orthocentre (H): Intersection of altitudes. H,O,C are collinear if OC is the altitude.
  • Right Angle in Circle: If HCA=90, AH is the diameter of the circumcircle.
  • Collinearity: Three points O,G,P are collinear if OP=kOG.
Strategy
  • Finding Vectors: Use section formula or linear combination with parameter t. Solve for t using dot product.
  • Projection Points: To find the projection of a point on a plane, construct a line through the point parallel to the normal, then intersect with the plane.
  • Geometric Relationships: Always check if one vector is a scalar multiple of another to prove collinearity or parallelism.
Required Skill
  • Scalar Product (Dot Product) & Vector Product (Cross Product).
  • Equation of a plane in scalar product form (rn=d).
  • Properties of triangles (orthocentre) and circles (diameter property).
