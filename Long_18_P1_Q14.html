Question Code: HKDSE_Math_Mensuration_Q14

14. Kelvin has 4 identical solid metal cubes of side 12 cm. From each of the cubes, Kelvin cuts off the largest square pyramid of same shape. All the remaining metals are melted and recast into 27 smaller identical solid square pyramids which are similar to the larger square pyramids cut off from the cubes.

(a) Find the height of a smaller square pyramid. (3 marks)

(b) Find the ratio of the total surface area of a smaller square pyramid to that of a larger square pyramid. (2 marks)

(c) Kelvin claims that the ratio in (b) will be different if the sides of the metal cubes used at the beginning are not 12 cm. Do you agree? Explain your answer. (3 marks)

Solution

Part (a): Find the height of a smaller square pyramid

Step 1: Analyze the "Largest Square Pyramid"
For a square pyramid cut from a cube of side 12 cm to be the "largest", its base must cover the face of the cube, and its height must equal the height of the cube.

Diagram 1: The largest pyramid shares the base and height of the cube.
  • Base Area of Large Pyramid = 1212=144 cm2
  • Height of Large Pyramid (H) = 12 cm

Volume of one Large Pyramid (VL): VL=13Base AreaHeight=13(122)(12)=13(1728)=576 cm3

Step 2: Calculate Remaining Volume
Volume of one Cube = 123=1728 cm3.
Volume remaining from one cube = 1728576=1152 cm3.
Careful: There are 4 cubes in total.

Total Volume of Remaining Metal (Vtotal): Vtotal=41152=4608 cm3

Step 3: Calculate Volume of Smaller Pyramids
This metal is recast into 27 smaller pyramids. Let VS be the volume of one smaller pyramid. 27VS=4608 VS=460827170.67 cm3

Step 4: Use Properties of Similar Figures
The smaller pyramids are similar to the large ones.

Diagram 2: Comparing the heights of similar pyramids.
VSVL=(height of small (h)Height of large (H))3 4608/27576=(h12)3 827=(h12)3 Taking the cube root of both sides: 23=h12 h=1223=8 cm

Concept Note: Similar Solids Remember the "Cube Law" for similar figures. If two solids are similar with linear ratio l1l2=k, then their volume ratio is V1V2=k3. Always look for the word "similar" in mensuration questions.
Part (b): Find the ratio of total surface areas

We already found the ratio of the corresponding lengths (heights) in Part (a): Linear Ratio (k)=hH=812=23

For similar solids, the ratio of their corresponding areas (including Total Surface Area) is the square of the linear ratio (k2). Ratio of Areas=k2=(23)2=49

Answer: The ratio is 4:9.

Common Trap Do not waste time calculating the actual surface area (slant heights, base areas, etc.). Since the question asks for a ratio and the figures are similar, simply square the length ratio.
Part (c): Kelvin's Claim

Let the side length of the cubes be L instead of 12.

  1. Volume of large pyramid (VL) = 13L3.
  2. Remaining volume per cube = L313L3=23L3.
  3. Total remaining volume (4 cubes) = 423L3=83L3.
  4. Volume of one small pyramid (VS) = Total27=8L3327=881L3.
  5. Ratio of volumes VSVL=881L313L3=827.

The volume ratio is constant (827), regardless of L.
Therefore, the linear ratio is always 8273=23.
Consequently, the area ratio is always (23)2=49.

Since the ratio remains 4:9 regardless of the side length L, Kelvin's claim is incorrect.

Conclusion: Disagree.

Notes & Strategies

Below are the specific skills and formulas required to solve this question efficiently.

Notes & Formulas
  • Volume of Pyramid: V=13Base AreaHeight
  • Similarity Formulas: Given two similar figures with linear ratio k:
    • Ratio of Lengths = k
    • Ratio of Areas = k2
    • Ratio of Volumes = k3
  • "Largest" Cut: Implies Height of Pyramid = Side of Cube.
Strategy
  1. Visualize the Cut: Understand that cutting the "largest" pyramid leaves exactly 23 of the cube's volume.
  2. Use Ratios Early: Instead of calculating massive numbers like 4608, you can often work purely with fractions of L3. This makes Part (c) obvious.
  3. Check Similarity: Always verify if objects are defined as "similar" before applying k2 or k3.
