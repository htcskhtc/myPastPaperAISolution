Question Code: DSE25PI_Q03

3. Let m and n be two numbers. The sum of 2m and 3n is 999 while the ratio of m to n is 8:7.
Find n.(3 marks)

Solution

Pedagogical Note (The k-method): When dealing with ratios like A:B=x:y, it is often mathematically cleaner to express the variables in terms of a constant k (e.g., A=xk,B=yk) rather than dealing with fractions (e.g., A=xyB). This avoids complex denominators in the subsequent substitution.
Given 2m+3n=999 and the ratio m:n=8:7. Let m=8k,n=7k for some number k. Substitute into 2m+3n=999: 2(8k)+3(7k)=999 16k+21k=999 37k=999 k=99937=27.
Common Trap: A very common mistake is stopping here and declaring the answer as 27. The question asks for n, not k. Always double-check what variable the question requires.
Hence, n=7k=7(27)=189. n=189

Notes & Strategy

Notes:

  • The "k-method" is a fundamental technique in the HKDSE Algebra curriculum for handling ratio problems.
  • Alternative Method: One could write m=87n and substitute into 2(87n)+3n=999. This involves fractions (16n/7+21n/7=999), which increases the risk of calculation errors compared to the integer-based k-method.

Strategy:

  1. Translate the word problem into mathematical equations.
  2. Convert the ratio m:n=8:7 into linear forms m=8k and n=7k.
  3. Substitute these into the sum equation to create an equation with only one variable (k).
  4. Solve for k.
  5. Substitute back to find the specific variable asked for (n).

Required Skill:

  • Formulating algebraic equations from text.
  • Handling ratios and proportions.
  • Solving linear equations in one unknown.

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes
  • Ratio Definition: If a:b=x:y, then a=xk and b=yk is the most robust substitution.
  • Verification: You can check your answer by finding m (827=216) and verifying 2(216)+3(189)=432+567=999.
Strategy Standard Substitution for Ratios:
