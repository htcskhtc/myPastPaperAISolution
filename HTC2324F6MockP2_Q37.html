37. Consider the following system of inequalities: {y7,7x+16y700,7x+9y700. Let R be the region which represents the solution of the above system of inequalities. Find the range of values of k such that the least value of 14x+ky is 140, where (x,y) is a point lying in R.

  • B. k18
  • C. k32

Solution

Step 1: Identify the Boundary Lines and Vertices

To analyze the region R, we first determine the boundary lines and their intersection points (vertices of the region).

The boundary lines are:

  1. L1:y=7
  2. L2:7x+16y70=07x+16y=70
  3. L3:7x+9y70=07x+9y=70

Step 2: Find the Intersection Points (Vertices)

Intersection of L1 and L2:
Substitute y=7 into 7x+16y=70: 7x+16(7)=707x+112=707x=42x=6 Vertex P1=(6,7).

Intersection of L1 and L3:
Substitute y=7 into 7x+9y=70: 7x+9(7)=707x+63=707x=7x=1 Vertex P2=(1,7).

Intersection of L2 and L3:
Solving the system: {7x+16y=707x+9y=70 Subtract the second equation from the first: 7y=0y=0 Substitute y=0 into 7x=70: x=10 Vertex P3=(10,0).

The region R is the triangle with vertices (6,7), (1,7), and (10,0). Checking a test point (e.g., (0,7)) confirms these boundaries enclose the valid region.

Step 3: Evaluate the Objective Function

Let Z=14x+ky. We are given that the least value of Z is 140. We evaluate Z at the vertices:

  • At P3(10,0): Z=14(10)+k(0)=140.
  • At P1(6,7): Z=14(6)+k(7)=84+7k.
  • At P2(1,7): Z=14(1)+k(7)=14+7k.
Concept Note: In linear programming, the maximum or minimum value of a linear function over a convex polygon always occurs at one of the vertices (or along a boundary edge connecting two vertices).

Step 4: Establish Inequalities for k

For 140 to be the least value, the value of Z at the other vertices must be greater than or equal to 140.

Condition 1 (Vertex P1): 84+7k1407k224k32

Condition 2 (Vertex P2): 14+7k1407k126k18

Step 5: Conclusion

Both conditions must be satisfied simultaneously. k32ANDk18k32 Therefore, the range of values of k is k32.

The correct option is C.

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes
  • Linear Programming Fundamental Theorem: The optimal value (max or min) of a linear objective function Z=Ax+By subject to linear constraints always occurs at a vertex of the feasible region.
  • If the optimal value occurs at two adjacent vertices, it occurs at every point on the line segment joining them.
  • Always verify which side of the line satisfies the inequality by testing a point (e.g., origin (0,0)).
Strategy
  1. Vertex Method: Convert inequalities to equations to identify boundary lines.
  2. Solve simultaneous equations to find the coordinates of all vertices.
  3. Substitute vertex coordinates into the objective function.
  4. If the optimal value is given, set up inequalities comparing the value at each vertex to the given optimal value.
