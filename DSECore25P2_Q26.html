Question Code: DSE25PII_Q26
  1. The straight lines L1 and L2 are perpendicular to each other. The equations of L1 and L2 are 3x+4y20=0 and mx+ny20=0 respectively, where m and n are constants. Suppose that L1 and L2 cut the x-axis at the points A and B respectively. Let C be the point of intersection of L1 and L2. It is given that C lies above the x-axis. If the area of ABC is 6, find n.
    1. 12
    2. 9
    3. 9
    4. 12

Solution

Solution.

Given L1:3x+4y20=0,L2:mx+ny20=0, and L1L2.

(1) Perpendicular condition
Concept Note: For a general line Ax+By+C=0, the slope is AB. Two lines are perpendicular if the product of their slopes is 1 (i.e., m1m2=1).
Rewrite L1: 3x+4y20=0y=34x+5, so the slope of L1 is 34.
Rewrite L2: mx+ny20=0y=mnx+20n, so the slope of L2 is mn.
Since L1L2, (34)(mn)=13m4n=1m=43n.

(2) Find A and B
Strategy: To find the x-intercept of any graph, substitute y=0 into its equation and solve for x.
A is the x-intercept of L1. Put y=0: 3x20=0x=203A(203,0).
B is the x-intercept of L2. Put y=0: mx20=0x=20m=2043n=15nB(15n,0).
Hence AB=203(15n)=203+15n.

(3) Find y-coordinate of C
Let C be the intersection of L1 and L2. Solve {3x+4y=20,mx+ny=20. Eliminating x, the y-coordinate is yC=6020m3n4m. Substitute m=43n: 3n4m=3n4(43n)=253n, 6020m=6020(43n)=60+803n. So yC=60+803n253n=36+16n5n=4(4n+9)5n.
Common Trap: Do not ignore conditions given in the question! The condition "C lies above the x-axis" means yC>0. This will be used to reject the extraneous root later.
Given C lies above the x-axis, yC>0, hence 4n+9n>0. Now AB=203+15n=20n+453n=5(4n+9)3n. Since 4n+9n>0, it follows that AB>0.

(4) Use the area condition
Since A and B lie on the x-axis, the height of ABC is yC. Thus 6=12AByC=125(4n+9)3n4(4n+9)5n. Simplifying, 6=2(4n+9)23n2(4n+9)2=9n2. Hence 4n+9=3nor4n+9=3n. So n=9orn=97. But yC=4(4n+9)5n and yC>0. For n=97, we have 4n+9>0 and n<0, giving yC<0, a contradiction.
Therefore, n=9.
Answer: B.
