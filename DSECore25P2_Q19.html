Question 19

19.\; In the figure, ABCD is a square. Let E be a point such that CDEF is a rhombus, where F is a point lying on AC produced. DF is produced to the point G such that AFBG. Which of the following are true?
  1. DF=FG
  2. BFGDEF
  3. ABG+BFD=180
  1. I and II only
  2. I and III only
  3. II and III only
  4. I, II and III

Optional caption

Solution

Let the side length of the square be s.

Key facts

  • ABCD is a square ABCD,BCAD,ABBC, and BC=CD=s.
  • F lies on AC produced A,C,F are collinear and CF is the extension of CA beyond C.
  • CDEF is a rhombus CD=DE=EF=FC. In particular, CF=CD=s. Also a rhombus is a parallelogram, so CDEF,CFDE.
Concept Note: Identifying that CF=CD is the crucial first step. Because CD is a side of the square (s) and CD is also a side of the rhombus, it links the two shapes together, creating isosceles triangles that are essential for angle tracing.

Step 1: Angles at C

In a square, diagonal AC bisects the right angle at C, hence BCA=ACD=45. Since CF is the extension of CA, BCF=180BCA=18045=135, DCF=180ACD=18045=135. So, BCF=DCF=135.

Step 2: Two isosceles triangles give 22.5

(a) Triangle BCF

Because BC=CD=s (square) and CF=CD=s (rhombus), we have BC=CF. Thus BCF is isosceles with vertex angle BCF=135, so the base angles are BFC=CBF=1801352=22.5.

(b) Triangle DCF

Also DC=CF=s, so DCF is isosceles with vertex angle DCF=135, hence CFD=CDF=1801352=22.5.
Common Trap: A common mistake is assuming BFD is 45 without calculating the components. While it turns out to be 45 here (22.5+22.5), you must strictly derive it from the two separate isosceles triangles BCF and DCF.

Step 3: Prove statement III

At F, ray FC lies between FB and FD, so BFD=BFC+CFD=22.5+22.5=45. Now AF is the same line as AC, and BGAFBGAC. Angle ABG is the angle between BA and a line parallel to AC. Since the angle between AB and AC is 45, the angle between BA (the opposite ray of AB) and AC is ABG=18045=135. Therefore ABG+BFD=135+45=180, so III is true.

Step 4: Prove statement II (BFGDEF)

Since D,F,G are collinear, FG is the extension of FD. Hence BFG=180BFD=18045=135. In the rhombus (a parallelogram), opposite angles are equal. Since DCF=135, we get DEF=135. Thus BFG=DEF=135. Next, BGAFACCF, and also DECF. Hence BGDE. Therefore BGF=(BG,GF)=(DE,DF)=EDF. But EDF is the angle between DE (parallel to CF) and DF. Since CFD=22.5 and DECF, EDF=22.5, so BGF=EDF. Hence BFG=DEF,BGF=EDF which implies BFGDEF. So II is true.

Step 5: Prove statement I (DF=FG)

From the similarity in Step 4, in BFG we have BFG=135,BGF=22.5, so the remaining angle is GBF=18013522.5=22.5. Hence GBF=BGF, so BFG is isosceles and (1)BF=FG. Now compare BCF and DCF:
  • BC=CD (square),
  • CF is common,
  • BCF=DCF=135 (Step 1).
So BCFDCF by SAS, giving (2)BF=DF. From (1) and (2), DF=BF=FGDF=FG. So I is true.

Conclusion

I, II and III are all true. Therefore the correct option is D.

Important Notes

Notes:
  • Properties of Square & Rhombus: Recall that a square has equal sides and interior angles of 90. A rhombus has equal sides. The connection point C shares sides CD and BC from the square, and CD and CF from the rhombus. This implies BC=CD=CF, creating crucial isosceles triangles.
  • Angle Tracing: The diagonal of a square bisects the corner angles (45). Angles on a straight line add to 180. These two facts allow us to find BCF=135.
Strategy:
  • Identify "Hidden" Isosceles Triangles: Whenever you have shared sides between regular polygons (like squares and rhombi), look for vertices equidistant from a central point. Here, C is the pivot point where BC=CF=CD.
  • Use Parallel Lines for Similarity: The problem states AFBG. This immediately suggests examining alternate angles or corresponding angles to prove triangle similarity (BFGDEF).
Required Skill:
  • Geometric Deductive Reasoning: Ability to chain properties of specific shapes to find unknown angles.
  • Similarity Proofs: Recognizing AA (Angle-Angle) conditions for similarity in complex figures.
  • Handling Collinear Points: Using the property of straight lines to find supplementary angles (180).

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes
  • Equality of Sides: Key realization is BC=CD (Square) and CD=CF (Rhombus) BC=CF.
  • Angle Properties: Diagonals of a square bisect angles (45). Angles on a straight line sum to 180.
  • Parallelism: Rhombus sides are parallel (DECF). Given BGAF.
Strategy
  1. Mark Lengths: Denote side length as s and mark all segments of length s (AB,BC,CD,DA,DE,EF,FC).
  2. Calculate Vertex Angles: Find BCF and DCF using the square's diagonal property.
  3. Solve Base Angles: Use the Isosceles Triangle Theorem on BCF and DCF to find small angles at F.
  4. Prove Similarity: Match angles in BFG and DEF using parallel line properties.
