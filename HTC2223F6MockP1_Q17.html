HKDSE Mathematics Solution

Question 17
  1. Let α and β be the roots of the equation x2−11x+30=k, where α>β and k is a real constant.
    1. Express (α−β)2 in terms of k. (3 marks)
    2. Suppose that T(n) is the nth term of an arithmetic sequence, where T(2)=α−β,T(3)=k+2andT(4)=2k−1. Find the greatest value of n such that 2T(1)×2T(2)×2T(3)×⋯×2T(n)<8100. (4 marks)

Solution

Part (a): Roots of Quadratic Equation

Rearrange the given equation into standard form ax2+bx+c=0:

x2−11x+(30−k)=0

From the sum and product of roots properties:

α+β=−−111=11αβ=30−k1=30−k
Common Trap: Many students confuse α2−β2 with (α−β)2. Remember that (α−β)2 relates directly to (α+β)2 via the identity below.

Using the algebraic identity relating sum and difference:

(α−β)2=(α+β)2−4αβ=(11)2−4(30−k)=121−120+4k=4k+1
Part (b): Arithmetic Sequence & Inequality

Step 1: Find the value of k

We are given T(2)=α−β. Since α>β, α−β must be positive.

T(2)=(α−β)2=4k+1

Given the sequence is Arithmetic (AS), the common difference d is constant:

T(3)−T(2)=T(4)−T(3)⟹2T(3)=T(2)+T(4)

Substituting the expressions in terms of k:

2(k+2)=4k+1+(2k−1) 2k+4=4k+1+2k−1 5=4k+1

Square both sides:

25=4k+1⟹24=4k⟹k=6
Check: Put k=6 back into 4k+1. 25=5, which is valid. Always ensure the value inside the square root is non-negative.

Step 2: Define the Arithmetic Sequence

T(3)=6+2=8T(4)=2(6)−1=11

Common difference d=T(4)−T(3)=11−8=3.

Find the first term T(1):

T(1)=T(2)−d=5−3=2

Step 3: Solve the Inequality

The inequality is:

2T(1)×2T(2)×⋯×2T(n)<8100

Using index laws am×an=am+n and 8=23:

2T(1)+T(2)+⋯+T(n)<(23)100 2S(n)<2300
Concept Note: Since the base 2>1, the inequality sign remains the same for the exponents. We need the Sum of AS S(n)<300.

Using the sum of AS formula S(n)=n2[2a+(n−1)d] with a=2,d=3:

n2[2(2)+(n−1)(3)]<300 n2[4+3n−3]<300 n(3n+1)2<300 3n2+n<600 3n2+n−600<0

Find roots of 3n2+n−600=0 using quadratic formula:

n=−1±12−4(3)(−600)2(3)=−1±72016 n≈−1±84.85876

Taking the positive root, we get n≈13.9765.

Since the quadratic expression 3n2+n−600<0 corresponds to the region between the roots, and n>0, we have:

0<n<13.9765

Since n must be an integer, the greatest value is:

n=13

Notes & Strategies

  • Key Identity: (α−β)2=(α+β)2−4αβ is essential for problems involving roots difference.
  • Arithmetic Mean: For any three consecutive terms a,b,c in an AS, 2b=a+c.
  • Logarithms/Indices: Always convert different bases (like 2 and 8) to a common prime base before comparing exponents.

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes & Formulas
  • Quadratic Roots: Sum α+β=−b/a, Product αβ=c/a.
  • Identity: (α−β)2=(α+β)2−4αβ.
  • AS Sum: S(n)=n2(2a+(n−1)d).
  • Indices: xa⋅xb=xa+b and (xa)b=xab.
Strategy
  1. For Q17(a), express coefficients in terms of k immediately.
  2. For Q17(b), link the value of α−β to T(2). Use the property 2T(3)=T(2)+T(4) to find k.
  3. Convert the product of powers into a summation of exponents (S(n)).
  4. Solve the resulting quadratic inequality for integer n.
