  1. A sum of $40,000 is deposited at an interest rate of 3% per annum for 5 years, compounded half-yearly. Find the amount correct to the nearest dollar.
    1. $46,000.
    2. $46,371.
    3. $46,422.
    4. $46,465.

Solution

We use the compound interest formula: A=P(1+rn)nt, where

  • P=40000 dollars (the principal),
  • r=0.03 (annual interest rate 3%),
  • n=2 (interest is compounded half-yearly, i.e. 2 times per year),
  • t=5 years.
Concept Note & Common Trap:
The value of n is determined by the compounding frequency.
  • Half-yearly: n=2 (2 periods per year)
  • Quarterly: n=4 (4 periods per year)
  • Monthly: n=12 (12 periods per year)
A common mistake is using n=1 or forgetting to divide r by n inside the bracket.

Substitute the values into the formula: A=40000(1+0.032)25=40000(1+0.015)10=40000(1.015)10.

Compute (1.015)10: (1.015)101.160540825.

Therefore, A400001.16054082546421.633.

Correct to the nearest dollar, the amount is A$46422.

Hence, the correct option is (C) $46422.

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes
  • Compound Interest Formula: A=P(1+rn)nt or A=P(1+R%)n where R is period rate.
  • Compounding Periods (n): Yearly (n=1), Half-yearly (n=2), Quarterly (n=4), Monthly (n=12).
  • Principal (P): The initial amount deposited or borrowed.
Strategy
  • Identify Variables: Explicitly write down P,r,n,t before plugging them into the calculator to avoid substitution errors.
  • Check Units: Ensure rate r and time t correspond to the same unit (usually years) before applying n.
  • Calculator Use: Learn to key in the entire expression 40000(1+0.03/2)(25) in one go to maximize precision.
