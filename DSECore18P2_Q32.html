HKDSE Mathematics (Compulsory Part) Solution

Question Code: DSE18PII_Q32
  1. The figure shows the graph of y=logax and the graph of y=logbx on the same rectangular coordinate system, where a and b are positive constants. If a vertical line cuts the graph of y=logax, the graph of y=logbx and the x-axis at the points A, B and C respectively, which of the following is/are true?
    1. a>1
    2. a>b
    3. ABBC=logaba
    1. I only
    2. II only
    3. I and III only
    4. II and III only
Solution
Let the vertical line be x=k, where k>1 (since the intersection points are to the right of the y-axis, and log is undefined for x0).
Then the intersections with the three graphs are A(k,logak),B(k,logbk),C(k,0).
Statement I: a>1
From the figure, the graph of y=logax is increasing as x increases.
A logarithmic function y=logax is increasing if and only if the base a>1.
Hence Statement I is true.
Concept Note: Logarithmic Graphs For y=logax:
  • If a>1, the curve goes up (increasing).
  • If 0<a<1, the curve goes down (decreasing).
Since the upper curve goes up, its base a must be greater than 1.

Statement II: a>b
At x=k, looking at the figure, the point A is above point B. The upper curve is y=logax and the lower curve is y=logbx, so logak>logbk. Using the change of base formula: logak=lnklna,logbk=lnklnb. Since k>1, we have lnk>0. Therefore, we can divide both sides of the inequality by lnk without flipping the sign: lnklna>lnklnb1lna>1lnb Since a>1 and (from the graph) b>1, both lna and lnb are positive. Taking the reciprocal flips the inequality sign: lna<lnba<b. Thus a>b is false and Statement II is false.
Common Trap & Alternative Method Trap: Students often think "higher curve = larger base". This is not always true for logarithms!
Alternative Strategy (Horizontal Line Test): Draw a horizontal line y=1.
  • The curve y=logax hits y=1 when x=a (since logaa=1).
  • The curve y=logbx hits y=1 when x=b (since logbb=1).
From the graph, the lower curve (y=logbx) is "flatter" and stretches further to the right before reaching height 1. Thus, b is further to the right than a, meaning b>a.

Statement III: ABBC=logaba
The vertical distances (y-coordinates) are: AB=yAyB=logaklogbk,BC=yByC=logbk0=logbk. Therefore ABBC=logaklogbklogbk. Using change of base to natural logs (ln): logak=lnklna,logbk=lnklnb. Substitute these into the fraction: ABBC=lnklnalnklnblnklnb=(lnklnalnklnb)lnblnk=(1lna1lnb)lnb(cancel lnk)=lnblna1=lnblnalna=ln(ba)lna Recall the change of base formula in reverse: lnXlnY=logYX. Here X=ba and Y=a. =loga(ba). Hence Statement III is true.
Therefore Statements I and III are true, and the correct answer is C.
Exam Strategies for this Question

1. Comparing Bases of Logarithms:
When comparing a and b in graphs of y=logax and y=logbx:

  • If the graphs are increasing, a,b>1.
  • If the graphs are decreasing, 0<a,b<1.
  • Quick Check: Draw the horizontal line y=1. The intersection with y=logax is at x=a. The intersection with y=logbx is at x=b. Whichever intersection is further right corresponds to the larger base.

2. Change of Base Formula:
This is essential for Statement III. Memorize: logab=lnblnaorlogab=logcblogca Use this to convert all terms to a common base (like e or 10) to simplify complex fractions.

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes
  • Log Properties: Function is increasing if base >1, decreasing if base is between 0 and 1.
  • Change of Base: logax=lnxlna. Useful for algebraic manipulation involving different bases.
  • Log Laws: lnxlny=ln(xy).
Strategy
  • Visual Comparison: Use the "Horizontal Line Method" (y=1) to quickly compare the magnitude of bases a and b without doing algebra.
  • Algebraic Verification: For ratio problems (like Statement III), convert everything to natural logs (ln) or a common base to simplify.
Required Skill
  • Properties of Logarithmic Graphs.
  • Algebraic manipulation of Logarithms (Change of Base).
  • Inequality logic (flipping signs when taking reciprocals of positive numbers).
