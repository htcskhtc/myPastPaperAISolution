Question 20 (a)
ABCD is a quadrilateral with ABC+ADC=180. Describe the relationship between the two circumcentres of ABC and ABD. (2 marks)
Step 1: Identify Concyclic Points

Since the sum of a pair of opposite angles is 180 (i.e., ABC+ADC=180), the quadrilateral ABCD is a cyclic quadrilateral.

This implies that points A,B,C, and D all lie on the same circle.

Concept Note: Cyclic Quadrilaterals If opposite angles sum to 180, the vertices are concyclic. This means there is only ONE unique circle that passes through all four vertices.
Step 2: Conclusion

Since A,B,C,D lie on the same circle, the circumcircle of ABC is identical to the circumcircle of ABD.

Therefore, their circumcentres are the same point (coincident).

Notes / Concepts
  • Concyclic Points: If opposite angles sum to 180, quadrilateral is cyclic.
  • Right-Angled Triangle Circumcentre: ALWAYS located at the midpoint of the hypotenuse. No distance formula needed.
  • Perpendicular Bisector Method: Intersection of x=a and y=mx+c is much faster than solving circle equations (xh)2....
