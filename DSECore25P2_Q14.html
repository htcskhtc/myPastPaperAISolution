HKDSE Mathematics - Coordinate Geometry

Question Code: IMG_Q14

In the figure, the equation of the straight line L is px+qy=7. Which of the following is/are true?

I. p>7
II. q>7
III. q>p

  1. I only
  2. II only
  3. I and III only
  4. II and III only

Solution Analysis

Step 1: Determine the Intercepts

First, we convert the given equation px+qy=7 into the intercept form or simply find the x- and y-intercepts by setting variables to zero.

  • x-intercept: Set y=0.
    px=7x=7p
  • y-intercept: Set x=0.
    qy=7y=7q
Concept Note: Reading Graphs

In coordinate geometry problems, always look for reference values on the axes. Here, the number 1 is marked on both the x-axis and the y-axis. These are your critical benchmarks for inequality comparison.

Step 2: Compare Intercepts with the Graph

Now, look visually at the provided figure:

  1. The y-intercept is located above the mark y=1.
    This implies: y-intercept>1
    7q>1
    Since the line hits the positive y-axis, q must be positive. We can multiply by q without flipping the sign:
    7>q (or q<7)
  2. The x-intercept is located to the left of the mark x=1 (between 0 and 1).
    This implies: 0<x-intercept<1
    7p<1
    Since the line hits the positive x-axis, p must be positive.
    7<p (or p>7)
Common Trap

Students often flip inequality signs incorrectly. Remember: if Ax>1 and x is positive, then A>x. If Ax<1 and x is positive, then A<x. Always double-check the visual position relative to the number 1.

Step 3: Evaluate the Statements

Let's check the options based on our findings (p>7 and q<7):

  • Statement I: p>7
    This matches our finding from the x-intercept. TRUE.
  • Statement II: q>7
    We found q<7. FALSE.
  • Statement III: q>p
    Since p>7 and q<7, it logically follows that p is a large number and q is a small number. Therefore p>q.
    The statement claims q>p. FALSE.
Conclusion

Only Statement I is true.

Answer: A

Exam Strategy Note

Visual Elimination Method:
1. Identify the intercepts symbolically (7p,7q).
2. Compare them immediately to the unit "1" on the axes.
3. Establish the inequalities (p>7,q<7).
4. Transitivity: If p>7>q, then p>q. This usually solves "Option III" instantly without complex algebra.

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes to Memorize
  • Finding Intercepts: To find x-intercept, put y=0. To find y-intercept, put x=0.
  • Slope Formula: For General Form Ax+By+C=0, slope =A/B.
  • Inequalities: When inverting fractions (e.g., 1/x>1/y), the inequality sign flips if both x and y are positive (x<y).
Strategy
  • Check 1: Locate the value "1" on axes. Is the intercept greater or smaller?
  • Check 2: Determine the sign of constants. (Line in Q1, Q2, Q4 implies positive intercepts, thus p,q>0).
  • Check 3: Use transitive property. If a>k and b<k, then a>b.
Required Skill
  • Converting linear equations to intercept form.
  • Interpreting inequalities from graphs.
  • Logical deduction for multiple-choice elimination.
