Question 14

14. In the figure, the equation of the straight line L is

px+qy=7.

Which of the following is/are true?

  1. p>7
  2. q>7
  3. q>p
  1. I only
  2. II only
  3. I and III only
  4. II and III only
Step-by-Step Solution

The equation of the straight line L is:

px+qy=7

First, we need to find the intercepts on the coordinate axes to compare them with the values in the graph.

Concept Note Intercepts: The x-intercept is the point where the line crosses the horizontal axis (where y=0). The y-intercept is where it crosses the vertical axis (where x=0).

1. Finding Intercepts:

  • For the x-axis, put y=0: px=7â‡’x=7p Thus, the x-intercept is 7p.
  • For the y-axis, put x=0: qy=7â‡’y=7q Thus, the y-intercept is 7q.

2. Analyzing from the Graph:

Statement I:

From the figure, the line cuts the positive x-axis between 0 and 1. This means the x-intercept is a proper fraction.

0<7p<1

Since the intercept is positive, p must be positive (p>0). We can multiply by p without flipping the sign:

7<pâ‡’p>7

Therefore, Statement I is true.

Statement II:

From the figure, the line cuts the y-axis at a value greater than 1.

7q>1

Since the intercept is positive in the first quadrant, q must be positive (q>0).

Common Trap Inequality Direction: When solving 7q>1, multiplying by q gives 7>q, which reads as "7 is greater than q" or "q is less than 7". Don't confuse q<7 with q>7.
7>qâ‡’q<7

Therefore, Statement II (q>7) is false.

Statement III:

We now combine our findings:

  • From (I), we have p>7.
  • From (II), we have q<7.

By the transitive property of inequality:

q<7<pâ‡’q<p

The statement claims q>p, which contradicts our finding.

Therefore, Statement III (q>p) is false.

Conclusion:

Only Statement I is true.

The correct answer is A.

Key Notes
  • Intercept Method: Always solve for intercepts symbolically (x=7/p, y=7/q) before looking at the numbers on the axes.
  • Visual Cues: Pay close attention to unit markings (like "1") on axes. Being "between 0 and 1" vs "above 1" is the key to solving the inequality.
  • Handling Inequalities: Remember that if kx>1 (where x,k>0), then x<k.
Strategy
  • Check 1: Locate the value "1" on axes. Is the intercept greater or smaller?
  • Check 2: Determine the sign of constants. (Line in Q1, Q2, Q4 implies positive intercepts, thus p,q>0.)
  • Check 3: Use transitive property. If a>k and b<k, then a>b.
