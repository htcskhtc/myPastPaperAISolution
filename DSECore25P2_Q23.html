Question Code: DSE25PII_Q23

The figure shows the quadrilateral ABCD. If ABC+ADC=ACD=BAC=90, which of the following must be true?

A. tanACB=ABAD
B. tanACB=ABCD
C. tanADC=BCAD
D. tanADC=BCCD

Solution

Given ABC+ADC=ACD=BAC=90.

Step 1: Use angle sum in right ABC

Since BAC=90, ABC is right-angled at A. Hence (1)ABC+ACB=90. But ABC+ADC=90, so comparing with (1) gives (2)ACB=ADC.

Concept Note: Angle Chasing When multiple angles sum to 90, look for "complementary pairs". Here, both ACB and ADC are complementary to ABC, making them equal.

Step 2: Relate CAD to ABC using right ACD

Since ACD=90, ACD is right-angled at C, so (3)CAD+ADC=90. Using (2), (4)CAD=90ADC=90ACB=ABC(by (1)).

Step 3: Prove ABCCAD

We have BAC=90=ACD, and by (2), ACB=ADC. Therefore ABCCAD (AA similarity).

Common Trap: Correspondence Order In similarity, vertex order is crucial.
B corresponds to A (derived in Step 2).
A corresponds to C (both 90).
C corresponds to D.
So, ABCCAD. Writing CAD as ACD would make ratios wrong.

A consistent correspondence is A in ABCC in CAD,CD,BA. Thus the corresponding sides are BCAD,ACCD,ABAC. Hence (5)BCAD=ACCD=ABAC.

Step 4: Compute tanADC

In right ACD (right angle at C), for angle ADC, (6)tanADC=oppositetextadjacent=ACCD. Using (5), ACCD=BCAD. Therefore tanADC=BCAD. So the statement that must be true is C.\ tanADC=BCAD.
