Question 03
  1. If a and b are constants such that (x+8)(x+a)+bx2+5a(x+3), then b=
    1. 14.
    2. 2.
    3. 2.
    4. 14.

Detailed Solution

We are given that (x+8)(x+a)+bx2+5a(x+3), where a and b are constants. We want to find the value of b.
Step 1: Expand both sides
Left-hand side (LHS): (x+8)(x+a)+b=(x(x+a)+8(x+a))+b=(x2+ax+8x+8a)+b=x2+(a+8)x+(8a+b). Right-hand side (RHS): x2+5a(x+3)=x2+5ax+15a. So the identity becomes x2+(a+8)x+(8a+b)x2+5ax+15a.
Common Trap: Equation vs. Identity Do not treat this as a quadratic equation where you move everything to one side to equal zero. This is an Identity (), meaning the coefficients of like terms on both sides must be exactly equal.
Step 2: Compare coefficients
Since the two polynomials are identically equal for all x, their corresponding coefficients must be equal.
  • Coefficient of x2: 1=1(already satisfied).
  • Coefficient of x: a+8=5a8=4aa=2.
  • Constant term: 8a+b=15ab=15a8a=7a. Substituting a=2 gives b=72=14.
Alternative Strategy: Substitution (Paper 2 Special) Since this is an identity true for all real values of x, you can substitute convenient values to find unknowns faster.

Try substituting x=0: (0+8)(0+a)+b=02+5a(0+3) 8a+b=15ab=7a This gives the relationship between a and b instantly, which matches our work above.
Therefore, b=14.
Exam Note In DSE Paper 2 (MC), speed is crucial. While expanding and comparing coefficients (Method 1) is the standard textbook approach, the Substitution Method (Method 2) is often faster and less prone to calculation errors. Always look for values of x that make brackets zero (e.g., if you saw (x2), put x=2).

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes
  • Identity (): Means the LHS is equal to the RHS for any value of x.
  • Property: If Ax2+Bx+CPx2+Qx+R, then A=P, B=Q, and C=R.
Strategy
  • Method 1 (Compare Coefficients): Expand both sides fully and equate the coefficient of x and the constant term separately.
  • Method 2 (Substitution): Substitute a "smart" value for x (like x=0, x=1, or a value that makes a bracket zero) into both sides to get simple linear equations for the constants.
