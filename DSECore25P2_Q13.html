Question 13
  1. Let an be the nth term of a sequence. If a2=3, a5=41 and an+2=2an+1+an for any positive integer n, then a6=
    1. 99
    2. 101
    3. 239
    4. 243
Solution:

Given the recurrence an+2=2an+1+an with a2=3 and a5=41, let a1=x.

Strategy Note: Since we have a "gap" between a2 and a5, we can't calculate forward directly. By setting a1=x, we can express all subsequent terms in terms of x, creating a linear equation to solve for the missing piece.

Then

a3=2a2+a1=23+x=6+x,a4=2a3+a2=2(6+x)+3=15+2x,a5=2a4+a3=2(15+2x)+(6+x)=36+5x.

Since a5=41, we have 36+5x=415x=5x=1. Hence a1=1.

Now that we have the "missing link" (x=1), we can calculate the required term:

a4=15+2(1)=17,a6=2a5+a4=241+17=99.

Therefore,

a6=99.
Universal Solver Note:
  • Concept: Linear Recurrence Relations.
  • Skill: Algebraic substitution to bridge gaps in a sequence.
  • Common Trap: Be careful when expanding brackets like 2(6+x). A sign error here will ruin the final answer.

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes
  • Recurrence Relations: Each term depends on previous terms (e.g., an+2 depends on an+1 and an).
  • To find a specific term like a6, you usually need the two immediately preceding terms (a5 and a4).
Strategy
  • Bridging the Gap: When given non-consecutive terms (e.g., a2 and a5), assign a variable (like x) to an early unknown term (a1 or a3).
  • Form an Equation: Express the later known term (a5) in terms of this variable to solve for it.
  • Forward Substitution: Once the variable is known, calculate terms step-by-step to reach the target (a6).
Required Skill
  • Solving Linear Equations.
  • Algebraic Expansion and Substitution.
  • Pattern Recognition in sequences.
