  1. A bag contains 10 five-dollar coins and 6 ten-dollar coins. May randomly draws 5 coins from the bag.
    1. Find the probability that May draws 3 five-dollar coins and 2 ten-dollar coins. (2 marks)
    2. Find the probability that the total value of the coins that May draws does not exceed $35. (2 marks)
Solution:

(a)

Total number of coins = 10+6=16.
Number of coins drawn = 5.

Concept Note: We use the combination formula Crn here because the order in which the coins are drawn does not matter. The denominator represents the total possible ways to choose 5 coins from 16.

(b)

Let x be the number of five-dollar coins drawn.
Then the number of ten-dollar coins drawn is 5x.

The total value of the coins V is: V=5(x)+10(5x) V=5x+5010x V=505x

We require the total value not to exceed $35, i.e., V35. 505x35 155x x3

Common Trap: Many students try to list combinations by guessing values. Solving the inequality x3 ensures you don't miss any cases. Remember that x cannot exceed the total number of coins drawn (5).

So, the possible values for x are 3,4, or 5.

  • Case 1 (x=3): 3 five-dollar coins and 2 ten-dollar coins. (Calculated in Part (a))
  • Case 2 (x=4): 4 five-dollar coins and 1 ten-dollar coin.
  • Case 3 (x=5): 5 five-dollar coins and 0 ten-dollar coins.
