Question 35 (Code: HTC2425F6MockP2_Q35)
  1. [35.] Let z=a+5a+73+i. If a is a purely imaginary number while z is a real number, then z=
    1. 1.
    2. 711.
    3. 79.
    4. 3.

Solution

Step 1: Define the unknown variable

Since a is a purely imaginary number, we can let a=ki, where k is a non-zero real number.

The expression becomes: z=ki+5ki+73+i

Step 2: Rationalize the denominator

To separate the real and imaginary parts, we must handle the fraction by multiplying the numerator and denominator by the conjugate of the denominator (3i).

Concept Note: Complex Number Division To simplify z1z2, always multiply the numerator and denominator by the conjugate of z2 (denoted as z2). If z2=x+yi, then z2=xyi.

ki+73+i=(ki+7)(3i)(3+i)(3i)=3kiki2+217i32+12=3kik(1)+217i10=k+21+(3k7)i10

Step 3: Substitute back into the expression for z

z=(ki+5)[(k+21)10+(3k7)i10]z=5k+2110+ki(3k7)i10z=(5k+2110)+i(k3k710)

Step 4: Use the given conditions

We are given that z is a real number. This implies that the imaginary part of z must be zero.

Im(z)=0k3k710=010k(3k7)=010k3k+7=07k=7k=1

So, a=i.

Step 5: Calculate the value of z

Now substitute k=1 back into the real part of z.

z=Re(z)z=5k+2110z=51+2110z=52010z=52z=3

The correct option is D.

Raw Notes & Strategy

Strategy:

  1. Substitution: Replace purely imaginary number a with ki.
  2. Simplification: Rationalize the complex fraction.
  3. Condition Application: Set Imaginary Part = 0 because z is real.
  4. Calculation: Solve for k and then find the Real Part.

Common Trap: Neglecting the negative sign when expanding the subtraction of the fraction, i.e., z=a+73+i. Students often forget to distribute the negative sign to both the real and imaginary parts of the fraction.

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes
  • Purely Imaginary Number: A number in the form bi where b0 and bR.
  • Real Number: A complex number where the imaginary part is 0.
  • Rationalizing Denominator: Multiply numerator and denominator by the conjugate (abi) to remove i from the denominator. Remember i2=1.
Strategy
  • Variable Definition: Explicitly write a=ki to avoid confusion between real and imaginary components.
  • Grouping: Always group terms into the form A+Bi. This makes applying conditions (like "z is real" or "z is purely imaginary") straightforward.
Required Skill
  • Arithmetic operations with complex numbers (Learning Objective 1.9).
  • Solving linear equations involving unknown coefficients.
