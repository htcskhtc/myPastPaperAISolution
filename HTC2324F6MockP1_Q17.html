Solution & Analysis

Part (a)(i) Find ABC

We are given the lengths of all three sides: AB=18, BC=11, AC=20. We need to find ABC (which is angle B).

Using the Cosine Rule:

cosB=AB2+BC2AC22(AB)(BC)
Concept Note: The Cosine Rule is perfect when you know SSS (Side-Side-Side) and need an angle. Remember, the side being subtracted in the numerator corresponds to the angle you are finding.

Part (a)(ii) Find the altitude from A to BC

Let h be the altitude from A to BC.

In the right-angled triangle formed by the altitude and side AB, we have:

sinB=OppositeHypotenuse=hAB
Alternative Method (Area): You could also calculate the area using Heron's formula and then use Area =12baseheight. However, using h=ABsinB is much faster since we just found B.
h=18sin(83.4746) h18(0.9935) h17.8837 cm

The altitude is 17.9 cm (correct to 3 significant figures).

Part (b) The Claim about 16

Analysis of the geometry:

  • The plate is tilted such that one edge is on the ground.
  • A vertical stick of height 5 cm fits under the plate.
  • For the stick to fit under the plate, the maximum height of the plate (which occurs at the vertex opposite the ground edge) must be at least 5 cm.

Let hmax be the vertical height of the highest vertex. Let halt be the altitude of the triangle corresponding to the edge on the ground. The relationship is:

sin=hmaxhalthmax=haltsin

For the stick to fit, we need hmax5. Therefore, we need:

haltsin5
Common Trap: Don't just check the altitude from part (a). The question says "only one of the edges lies on the ground". It doesn't specify which edge. To give John the best chance of being correct, we should test the scenario that yields the greatest height. This corresponds to the longest altitude (which corresponds to the shortest base).

Step 1: Identify the longest altitude.

The Area of the triangle is constant. Since Area =12basealtitude, the shortest base will have the longest altitude.

  • Base BC=11 (Shortest side) Altitude from A is the longest.
  • We calculated this altitude in (a)(ii): hA17.8837 cm.

Since BC is the shortest side, 17.8837 cm is indeed the maximum possible altitude (hmax_alt).

Step 2: Find the minimum required angle .

For the stick of height 5 cm to fit under the plate, the maximum height of the plate must be at least 5 cm.

Step 3: Conclusion.

The minimum angle required is approximately 16.24.

Since John claims can be 16, which is less than the minimum required (16<16.24), the stick cannot fit.

Answer: No, I do not agree.
