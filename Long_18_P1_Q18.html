Question 18

For any positive integer n, let an=19n57 and bn=3125mn, where m>1. It is given that the sum to infinity of the series b1+b2+b3+ is 12500.

(a) Find the value of m. (2 marks)

(b) Define zk=(a1+a2++ak)+(b1+b2++bk)i, where i=1. If zk is purely imaginary, find zk. (3 marks)

Solution

Part (a): Sum to Infinity of Geometric Sequence

The sequence bn is a geometric sequence defined by bn=3125mn.

First term a=b1=3125m.

Common ratio r=b2b1=3125/m23125/m=1m.

Common Trap Students often mistake the constant in the numerator (3125) for the first term. Remember to substitute n=1 to find the first term b1.

Using the sum to infinity formula S=a1r:

3125m11m=12500 3125m(11m)=12500 3125m1=12500 m1=312512500 m1=14 m=54 (or 1.25)
Part (b): Complex Numbers and Summation

Given zk=n=1kan+in=1kbn.

Since zk is purely imaginary, the Real Part must be zero.

Re(zk)=n=1kan=0
Concept Note Purely Imaginary: Real Part = 0 (a=0 in a+bi).
Real Number: Imaginary Part = 0 (b=0 in a+bi).

Analyze an=19n57. This is an arithmetic sequence with:

  • First term a1=19(1)57=38
  • Last term ak=19k57

Using the sum of arithmetic sequence formula Sk=k2(a1+ak):

k2[(38)+(19k57)]=0 k2(19k95)=0
