Question Code: DSE18PII_Q25

25. The equations of the straight lines L1 and L2 are 3xy+7=0 and 12x4y11=0 respectively.
Let P be a moving point in the rectangular coordinate plane such that the perpendicular distance from P to L1 is equal to the perpendicular distance from P to L2.
Find the equation of the locus of P.

  1. 8x24y17=0
  2. 8x24y+17=0
  3. 24x8y17=0
  4. 24x8y+17=0
Solution Breakdown

We are given the equations:

L1: 3xy+7=0 L2: 12x4y11=0
(1) Show that L1 and L2 are parallel

First, simplify the equation of L2 by dividing by 4:

12x4y11=012x4y114=03xy114=0

Hence we can write:

L1: 3xy+7=0 L2: 3xy114=0

Both lines have the same terms 3xy, which implies they are parallel with slope m=3.

Key Concept: Identifying Parallel Lines Two lines A1x+B1y+C1=0 and A2x+B2y+C2=0 are parallel if the ratio of their coefficients is equal (A1:B1=A2:B2). Always simplify the equations so the x and y coefficients match perfectly before comparing constant terms.
(2) Geometric Interpretation

The locus of points equidistant from two parallel lines is a third line that:

  • Is parallel to both original lines.
  • Lies exactly halfway between them.
Shortcut Strategy (Recommended for MC) If two lines are expressed as Ax+By+C1=0 and Ax+By+C2=0, the line equidistant from them is simply the "average" of the two: Ax+By+C1+C22=0 Using this formula saves finding intersections and midpoints!
(3) Calculation (Standard & Shortcut)

Using the derived form where coefficients match (3xy):

  • Constant for L1: C1=7
  • Constant for L2: C2=114

The constant for the locus line (Cnew) is the average:

Cnew=7+(114)2=2841142=1742=178

So the equation of the locus is:

3xy+178=0
Common Trap A common mistake is forgetting to convert the final equation back to the General Form (Ax+By+C=0) with integer coefficients, which is required by the multiple-choice options.

Multiply the entire equation by 8 to remove the fraction:

8(3x)8(y)+8(178)=0 24x8y+17=0
Answer: The equation of the locus of P is
24x8y+17=0
(Matches Option D)

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes to Memorize

  • Locus of Equidistance: The locus of points equidistant from two parallel lines is the parallel line bisecting the distance between them.
  • Condition for Parallelism: Slopes (m) are equal, or coefficients of x and y are proportional.
  • General Form: Ax+By+C=0.

Exam Strategy

  • Check Parallelism First: Before applying distance formulas, check if the lines are parallel. It simplifies the problem drastically.
  • Use the "Average" Shortcut: For parallel lines Ax+By+C1=0 and Ax+By+C2=0, the middle line is Ax+By+C1+C22=0. This is much faster than finding intersection points.

Required Skills

  • Manipulating linear equations (Slope-intercept vs General form).
  • Operations with fractions.
  • Comparing coefficients of line equations.
