Question Code: DSE20PII_Q14
  1. In the figure, PQRST is a pentagon, where all the measurements are correct to the nearest cm. Let A cm2 be the actual area of the pentagon. Find the range of values of A.
    1. 27.83A<31.83
    2. 44.75A<60.75
    3. 46.75A<63.25
    4. 48.25A<64.75
Optional caption
Solution
From the right-angle marks, PQQR, PQPT, and QRRS. Hence, we can regard the pentagon as a rectangle minus a right triangle.

Step 1: Form an area expression (no coordinates). The rectangle has width QR and height PQ, so its area is Area of rectangle=PQQR. The cut-off part at the top-right is a right triangle. Its: horizontal leg=QRPT,vertical leg=PQRS. Thus, Area of triangle=12(QRPT)(PQRS). Therefore, the actual area of the pentagon is A=PQQR12(QRPT)(PQRS). Expanding gives a convenient form: (1)A=PQQR12(QRPQQRRSPTPQ+PTRS)=12(PQ(QR+PT)+RS(QRPT)).
Concept Note: Area Calculation Strategy Why "Rectangle minus Triangle"? While you can split the pentagon into a rectangle (PQPT) and a trapezium (RS(QRPT)), the "Rectangle minus Triangle" method often yields simpler algebraic expressions. However, both methods are mathematically valid. The key is to obtain an expression where all variables (PQ,QR, etc.) have positive coefficients if possible, making it easier to see that the Area increases when any side length increases.


Step 2: Use the bounds from "nearest cm". Given the measurements are correct to the nearest cm: PQ=65.5PQ<6.5,QR=109.5QR<10.5,PT=54.5PT<5.5,RS=32.5RS<3.5. Also, throughout these ranges, QRPT>0 and PQ>RS.

Step 3: Find the minimum and maximum of A. From (1), A=12(PQ(QR+PT)+RS(QRPT)), which is increasing in each of PQ,QR,PT,RS (since QR+PT>0 and QRPT>0). Hence, Amin occurs at all lower bounds, and Amax occurs at all upper bounds (not inclusive).

Minimum: Amin=12(5.5(9.5+4.5)+2.5(9.54.5))=12(77+12.5)=44.75.

Maximum (not inclusive): Amax=12(6.5(10.5+5.5)+3.5(10.55.5))=12(104+17.5)=60.75. Since each upper bound is of the form "<", the value 60.75 is not attainable, so A<60.75.

Therefore, 44.75A<60.75 which corresponds to option B.

Notes & Strategy

Key Strategy:
  • Measurement Errors: For a measurement x correct to the nearest k, the actual value lies in the interval [xk2,x+k2). In this question, k=1 cm, so the error is 0.5 cm.
  • Propagation of Errors: To find the range of a calculated quantity (like Area), you must substitute the boundary values of the measurements into the formula.
  • Monotonicity Check: It is crucial to check if the Area function is increasing or decreasing with respect to the variables. In this case, since the area increases when any side length increases (positive coefficients in the expanded formula), Amin is found using all lower bounds, and Amax using all upper bounds.
Common Mistake:

Many students calculate the area using the nominal given values first (e.g., calculating Area using 6,10,5,3 to get a value Anom) and then try to apply the error bound (0.5) to the final result (i.e., Anom0.5A<Anom+0.5). This is incorrect because error propagates non-linearly through multiplication.

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes & Concepts
  • Measurement Interval: "Correct to the nearest N" means the value is in [MeasuredN2,Measured+N2).
  • Bounds Logic:
    • Lower Bound = Measured Value 0.5 precision.
    • Upper Bound = Measured Value +0.5 precision.
  • Area Function Behavior: Ensure that the area expression is monotonic (increasing/decreasing) for all variables before simply plugging in all lower or all upper bounds.
Strategy
  1. Step 1: Express the Area A in terms of the measured lengths (PQ,QR,PT,RS). Simplifying the expression is helpful.
  2. Step 2: Determine the Lower Limit (L) and Upper Limit (U) for each individual measurement.
  3. Step 3: Calculate Amin by substituting the Lower Limits (if Area is increasing).
  4. Step 4: Calculate Amax by substituting the Upper Limits.
  5. Step 5: Combine into the range AminA<Amax.
