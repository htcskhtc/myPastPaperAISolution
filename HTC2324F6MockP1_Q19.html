Question 19

The coordinates of the points P and Q are (60,0) and (36,t) respectively, where t>0. Denote the origin by O. Suppose that C and H are the circumcentre and the orthocentre of OPQ respectively.

  1. Express the coordinates of C and H in terms of t. (6 marks)
  2. It is given that O, C and H are collinear.
    1. Prove that t=48.
    2. Let R be a point lying on OP such that CR is perpendicular to OP. Denote the centroid of OPQ by G. Someone claims that the ratio of the area of GHQ to the area of GCR is 2:1. Do you agree? Explain your answer. (6 marks)

Detailed Solution

Part (a): Coordinates of Circumcentre C

The circumcentre C is the intersection of the perpendicular bisectors of the sides of the triangle.

Concept: To find the circumcentre, you only need to find the intersection of two perpendicular bisectors. Choose the simplest sides. Here, OP lies on the x-axis, making its perpendicular bisector very easy to find.

1. Perpendicular bisector of OP:
Midpoint of OP=(0+602,0+02)=(30,0).
Since OP is horizontal (slope = 0), its perpendicular bisector is the vertical line x=30.
Thus, the x-coordinate of C is 30. Let C=(30,yC).

2. Perpendicular bisector of OQ:
O=(0,0), Q=(36,t).
Midpoint of OQ=(0+362,0+t2)=(18,t2).
Slope of OQ, mOQ=t0360=t36.
Slope of perpendicular bisector = 1mOQ=36t.

Equation of perpendicular bisector of OQ: yt2=36t(x18)

Substitute x=30 (from the first bisector) into the equation: yCt2=36t(3018) yCt2=36t(12) yC=t2432t yC=t28642t

C=(30,t28642t)

Part (a): Coordinates of Orthocentre H

The orthocentre H is the intersection of the altitudes of the triangle.

1. Altitude from Q to OP:
Since OP lies on the x-axis, the altitude from Q(36,t) is simply the vertical line x=36.
Thus, the x-coordinate of H is 36. Let H=(36,yH).

2. Altitude from P to OQ:
Slope of OQ=t36.
The altitude passes through P(60,0) and is perpendicular to OQ.
Slope of altitude = 36t.

Equation of altitude: y0=36t(x60)

Substitute x=36 (from the first altitude) into the equation: yH=36t(3660) yH=36t(24) yH=864t

H=(36,864t)

Part (b)(i): Prove t=48

It is given that O(0,0), C(30,t28642t), and H(36,864t) are collinear.

This implies that the slope of OC is equal to the slope of OH.

Common Trap: Be careful with complex fractions. When equating slopes, write them out clearly before cross-multiplying to avoid algebraic errors.

Slope of OH=864t0360=86436t=24t

Slope of OC=t28642t0300=t286460t

Equating slopes: 24t=t286460t

Since t>0, we can multiply both sides by 60t: 24(60)=t2864 1440=t2864 t2=2304 t=48 or t=48

Since t>0, we have t=48.

R lies on OP (the x-axis) such that CROP. Since C=(30,15), the projection of C onto the x-axis is R=(30,0).

2. Analyze Lines HQ and CR:
H(36,18) and Q(36,48) share the same x-coordinate (x=36). Thus, HQ is a vertical line.
C(30,15) and R(30,0) share the same x-coordinate (x=30). Thus, CR is a vertical line.
Since both lines are vertical, HQCR.

3. Prove Similarity (GHQGCR):
Consider GHQ and GCR:

  • HGQ=CGR (vertically opposite angles)
  • GHQ=GCR (alternate interior angles, since HQCR)

The claim states the ratio is 2:1.
The claim is disagreed.

Important Coordinate Geometry Properties for Triangles:

  • Circumcentre (C): Intersection of perpendicular bisectors. Distance to all vertices is equal (OC=PC=QC).
  • Orthocentre (H): Intersection of altitudes.
  • Centroid (G): Intersection of medians. Average of coordinates: G=(x1+x2+x33,y1+y2+y33).
  • Euler Line: For any triangle, O,G,C,H (Circumcentre, Centroid, Orthocentre) lie on a straight line (if the origin is not the circumcentre, generally H,G,C are collinear).
Notes
  • Slope of perpendicular lines: m1m2=1.
  • Equation of vertical line: x=k; Horizontal line: y=k.
  • Area of Similar Triangles: Ratio of Areas = (Ratio of Sides)2.
  • Collinear points have equal slopes between any pair.
Strategy
  • Identify Similar Triangles: When finding area ratios involving parallel lines (like vertical altitudes), look for similarity first. It is often much faster than calculating coordinates of intersection points (like the Centroid G).
  • Perpendicular Bisectors: If a side is on an axis, its bisector is simply x=midpoint.
