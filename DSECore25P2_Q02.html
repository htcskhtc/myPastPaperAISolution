Question: DSE25PII_Q02
  1. 36(3m+4n)2=
    1. (6+3m+4n)(63m+4n).
    2. (6+3m+4n)(63m4n).
    3. (6+3m4n)(63m+4n).
    4. (6+3m4n)(63m4n).
Solution
We want to factor 36(3m+4n)2. Step 1: Recognize a difference of squares Note that 36=62, so we can write 36(3m+4n)2=62(3m+4n)2.
Key Concept: The Identity of Difference of Two Squares is a2b2=(a+b)(ab). Identifying the "squares" is the first crucial step.
This matches the identity a2b2=(a+b)(ab), where a=6,b=3m+4n. Thus, 62(3m+4n)2=(6+(3m+4n))(6(3m+4n)). Simplify each factor: 6+(3m+4n)=6+3m+4n, 6(3m+4n)=63m4n.
Common Trap: When subtracting a compound term like (3m+4n), you MUST distribute the negative sign to BOTH terms.
Incorrect: 6(3m+4n)63m+4n
Correct: 6(3m+4n)63m4n
Therefore, 36(3m+4n)2=(6+3m+4n)(63m4n). Step 2: Match with the options Comparing with the given options, (B)(6+3m+4n)(63m4n) is the correct factorization.
Examiner's Note & Strategy
  • Topic: Factorization of Polynomials (Identity: a2b2).
  • Core Skill: Applying the difference of two squares formula where one term is a bracketed expression (e.g., (3m+4n)2).
  • Strategy: Always rewrite the constant term as a square (e.g., 3662) to make the form A2B2 obvious.
  • Careless Mistake Prevention: The most common error in this question type is sign error during the expansion of (B). Always use brackets: (3m+4n)=3m4n.

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes
  • Memorize the Identity: a2b2(ab)(a+b).
  • Recognize perfect squares: 1,4,9,16,25,36,49,64,81,100...
Strategy
  1. Rewrite: Convert all terms to (something)2 form.
  2. Substitute: Let A=first term and B=second term.
  3. Expand Carefully: Pay strict attention to negative signs when B is a polynomial. A(x+y)=Axy.
Required Skill
  • Algebraic Manipulation.
  • Expansion of brackets with negative signs.
  • Pattern recognition (Difference of Squares).
