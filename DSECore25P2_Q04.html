Question DSE25PII_Q04
  1. If (3c+1)(d4)=2d(5c1), then c=
    1. 3d47d+12.
    2. 3d+47d12.
    3. 7d123d+4.
    4. 7d+123d4.
Solution

We are given the equation (3c+1)(d4)=2d(5c1).

Step 1: Expand both sides.

Left-hand side (LHS): (3c+1)(d4)=3c(d4)+1(d4)=3cd12c+d4.

Concept Note: When expanding (A+B)(C+D), ensure every term in the first bracket multiplies every term in the second bracket. FOIL method (First, Outer, Inner, Last) is a common mnemonic.

Right-hand side (RHS): 2d(5c1)=10cd2d.

So the equation becomes 3cd12c+d4=10cd2d.

Step 2: Bring all terms to one side.

Move all terms to the right-hand side: 0=10cd2d(3cd12c+d4).

Common Trap: Be extremely careful with signs when moving multiple terms or subtracting an entire expression. Notice how (3cd12c+d4) becomes 3cd+12cd+4.

Distribute the minus sign: 0=10cd2d3cd+12cd+4.

Combine like terms: 10cd3cd=7cd,2dd=3d, so 0=7cd+12c3d+4.

Thus, 7cd+12c3d+4=0.

Step 3: Factor to isolate c.

Key Strategy: To make c the subject, group ALL terms containing c on one side and move everything else to the other side.

Group the terms involving c: c(7d+12)(3d4)=0.

Hence, c(7d+12)=3d4.

Provided 7d+120, we can divide both sides by 7d+12: c=3d47d+12.

Step 4: Match with the options.

We have found c=3d47d+12, which corresponds to Option A.

Therefore, the answer is A. 3d47d+12.

HKDSE Expert Notes
  • Topic: Change of Subject / Formulas.
  • Core Procedure:
    1. Remove brackets (Expand).
    2. Clear fractions (if any) by multiplying by the denominator.
    3. Move all terms containing the target variable (here, c) to one side.
    4. Move all other terms to the opposite side.
    5. Factorize out the target variable.
    6. Divide by the bracket to isolate the variable.
  • Checking: You can quickly substitute simple numbers (e.g., d=1) to check if LHS matches RHS, though algebraic derivation is safer for Paper 2.
