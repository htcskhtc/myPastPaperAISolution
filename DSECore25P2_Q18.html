Question Code: DSE25PII_Q18

Question

  1. The figure shows the triangle WXY. Let Z be a point lying on WY such that WY:XY=XY:YZ. If WX=65 cm, WZ=25 cm and XZ=60 cm, then XY=
    1. 131 cm.
    2. 144 cm.
    3. 156 cm.
    4. 169 cm.

Solution

Given that WX=65 cm, WZ=25 cm and XZ=60 cm, we first check whether WXZ is right-angled:

252+602=625+3600=4225=652.
Concept Note: Converse of Pythagoras Theorem Whenever you are given three side lengths of a triangle, explicitly check if a2+b2=c2. If it holds true, the triangle is a right-angled triangle. This is a crucial hidden property often required to proceed.

Hence, WXZ is a right-angled triangle with a right angle at Z. Therefore, XZWY, and so XZY is also right-angled at Z.

Let YZ=y cm. Then WY=WZ+YZ=25+y.

We are given that WY:XY=XY:YZ. This implies (1)WYXY=XYYZXY2=WYYZ=(25+y)y.

Strategy Note: Algebraic Substitution We now have two unknowns (XY and y) and two relationships. One comes from the ratio given in the question (Eq. 1), and the other comes from the geometry (Pythagoras Theorem in XZY).

On the other hand, in the right-angled triangle XZY, (2)XY2=XZ2+YZ2=602+y2=3600+y2.

Equating (1) and (2), we obtain 3600+y2=y2+25y

Common Trap Notice how the y2 terms on both sides cancel out immediately. Do not rush to expand or move terms into a complex quadratic equation unless necessary. Look for cancellations first!
3600=25yy=360025=144.

Substituting y=144 into (2), XY2=602+1442=3600+20736=24336, so XY=24336=156.

Therefore, XY=156 cm.

156 cm

Notes & Key Concepts

Additional Insights
  • Geometric Mean Property: The relationship XY2=WYYZ often appears in similar triangles. Specifically, if WXYXZY, this ratio holds. The question gave this ratio directly, but in harder questions, you might need to prove similarity first.
  • Pythagorean Triples: Recognizing numbers like (5,12,13) scaled by 5 gives (25,60,65). Spotting these triples can speed up your calculation verification.

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes (Memorize)
  • Converse of Pythagoras Theorem: If a2+b2=c2, the angle opposite side c is 90.
  • Pythagoras Theorem: In a right-angled triangle, a2+b2=c2.
  • Ratio to Equation: A:B=C:DAB=CDAD=BC.
Strategy
  1. Check Geometry First: If 3 sides are given, always check for a right angle. This unlocks perpendicularity (XZWY).
  2. Define Variables: Let unknown lengths be y or x.
  3. Form Equations: Use geometric theorems (Pythagoras) and given conditions (Ratios) to form simultaneous equations.
  4. Solve: Equate expressions for a shared side (like XY2) to solve for the variable.
Required Skill
  • Handling Ratios (WY:XY=XY:YZ).
  • Application of Pythagoras Theorem and its Converse.
  • Solving simple linear equations derived from geometric contexts.
