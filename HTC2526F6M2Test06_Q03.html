HKDSE Mathematics Module 2 Solution

Question 3

Let O be the origin. The position vectors of A, B and C are 2ij, 5i+2j and i+4j respectively. It is given that point D lies on AB such that AD:DB=1:2. It is given that point E lies on AC such that AE:EC=1:2.

  1. Describe the geometric relationship between DE and BC.
  2. Let F be another distinct point in a rectangular coordinate plane. It is given that |DE|=|DF|+|FE| and |DF|=21013. Find FE. (5 marks)

Solution

Part (a)

Step 1: Find the position vectors OD and OE.

Concept Note: Section Formula If a point P divides the segment AB in the ratio r:s, then OP=sOA+rOBr+s.

Given AD:DB=1:2, applying the section formula for D:

OD=2OA+1OB1+2=2(2ij)+(5i+2j)3=4i2j+5i+2j3=9i3=3i

Given AE:EC=1:2, applying the section formula for E:

OE=2OA+1OC1+2=2(2ij)+(i+4j)3=4i2ji+4j3=3i+2j3=i+23j

Step 2: Calculate DE and BC.

DE=OEOD=(i+23j)3i=2i+23j BC=OCOB=(i+4j)(5i+2j)=6i+2j

Step 3: Compare the vectors to find the relationship.

Observe the relationship between the coefficients:

BC=6i+2j=3(2i+23j)=3DE

Conclusion:

Since BC=3DE, the vector BC is parallel to DE.

Therefore, DE is parallel to BC (DEBC) and the length of BC is 3 times the length of DE.

Part (b)

Step 4: Analyze the condition |DE|=|DF|+|FE|.

Concept Note: Degenerate Triangle Inequality The Triangle Inequality states |a|+|b||a+b|. Equality holds if and only if the vectors act in the same direction. Geometrically, for points D,F,E, if the distance |DE| equals the sum of distances |DF| and |FE|, then the points must be collinear, and F must lie on the line segment between D and E.

Since |DE|=|DF|+|FE|, point F lies on the line segment DE.

Consequently, the vector FE points in the same direction as the vector DE.

Common Trap: Direction Confusion Be careful with vector direction. Since F is between D and E, the vector from F to E (FE) points towards E, just like the vector from D to E (DE). If the question asked for EF, it would be the opposite direction.

Step 5: Calculate magnitudes.

Magnitude of DE=2i+23j:

|DE|=(2)2+(23)2=4+49=409=2103

Given |DF|=21013.

We find |FE|:

|FE|=|DE||DF|=210321013=13

Step 6: Determine FE.

Since FE is in the same direction as DE, we can use the ratio of their magnitudes to scale DE.

Ratio k=|FE||DE|=1/3(210)/3=1210

Thus:

FE=kDE=1210(2i+23j)=2210i+2610j=110i+1310j

Rationalizing the denominators (Optional but recommended):

FE=1010i+1030j

Notes & Key Takeaways

1. Properties of Similar Triangles / Section Formula:

In this problem, the ratios on the sides (1:2) were identical for both sides (AB and AC). This is a direct application of the converse of the Basic Proportionality Theorem (or Intercept Theorem), which guarantees that the line joining the dividing points is parallel to the third side.

2. Collinearity Condition:

The condition |AB|=|AC|+|CB| always implies that C lies strictly on the line segment AB. This is a crucial geometric interpretation of vector magnitudes.

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes (Formulas & Concepts)
  • Section Formula: OP=na+mbm+n for ratio m:n.
  • Parallel Vectors: aba=kb for some scalar k.
  • Triangle Inequality Equality: |u+v|=|u|+|v| iff u and v have the same direction.
Strategy (Procedural Steps)
  1. Use the section formula to find position vectors of dividing points relative to the origin.
  2. Construct displacement vectors (e.g., DE=OEOD) to compare directions.
  3. For part (b), use the magnitude relation to determine the relative position of points on a line.
  4. Use the ratio of magnitudes to scale the known vector to find the unknown vector segment.
Required Skill
  • Vector arithmetic (addition, subtraction, scalar multiplication).
  • Calculation of vector magnitude (modulus).
  • Geometric interpretation of vector equations.
  • Rationalization of surds (basic algebra).
