Question Code: DSE19PII_Q23

23. In the figure, the equation of the straight line L is ax+by+15=0. Which of the following are true?

  1. a>b
  2. a>3
  3. b>5
  1. I and II only
  2. I and III only
  3. II and III only
  4. I, II and III
Solution Analysis

The equation of the straight line L is ax+by+15=0.

1. Intercepts and sign of a and b.

Rewrite the equation in the form y=mx+c: by=ax15y=abx15b. Hence the slope of the line is m=ab.

Concept Note: Slope-Intercept Form Always convert the general form Ax+By+C=0 to the slope-intercept form y=mx+c to easily identify the slope m and y-intercept c. Here, slope m=A/B and y-intercept c=C/B.

From the diagram, the line is sloping downwards from left to right, so the slope is negative: ab<0ab>0, which means a and b have the same sign.

Next, consider the y-intercept. Putting x=0 in ax+by+15=0, we get by+15=0y=15b. From the diagram, the line cuts the y-axis between 0 and 3, i.e. (1)0<15b<3.

The left inequality 0<15b implies 15b>0b<0. Since a and b have the same sign, we also have a<0.

2. Condition from the x-intercept.

Putting y=0 in ax+by+15=0, we get ax+15=0x=15a. From the diagram, the line cuts the x-axis to the right of x=5, so (2)15a>5.

We already know a<0. Multiply both sides of (2) by a (a negative number), so the inequality sign reverses: 15<5a.

Common Trap: Inequality Signs When multiplying or dividing an inequality by a negative number (here, a<0), you MUST reverse the inequality sign (e.g., from > to <). Failing to do this is a very common mistake.

Divide both sides by 5: 3<a. Together with a<0, we obtain (3)3<a<0.

3. Condition from the y-intercept.

From (1), we use the right inequality: 15b<3. Since b<0, multiplying both sides by b reverses the inequality: 15>3b. Dividing both sides by 3, we get (4)5>bb<5.

4. Checking the statements.

From (3) and (4), we have 3<a<0andb<5.

  • I. a>b.

    Since a>3 and b<5, and 3>5, we have a>3>5>ba>b. So statement I is true.
  • II. a>3.

    This is exactly the inequality in (3), so statement II is true.
  • III. b>5.

    From (4), b<5, which contradicts b>5. So statement III is false.

Therefore, only statements I and II are true.

Answer: (A) I and II only.

Concept Notes & Key Strategies

1. General Equation of a Line: For Ax+By+C=0:
- Slope m=AB
- x-intercept = CA (Put y=0)
- y-intercept = CB (Put x=0)

2. Handling Inequalities:
Always determine the sign of the variables (like a and b here) before multiplying them across an inequality sign. If the variable is negative, flip the sign.

3. Visual Inspection:
- Line slopes down Slope <0.
- Line cuts positive y-axis y-int >0.
- Line cuts positive x-axis x-int >0.

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes

  • Golden Rule of Inequalities: Reverse the symbol when multiplying/dividing by a negative number.

Strategy
  1. Identify Signs: Determine the signs of a and b first using the slope direction and the sign of the y-intercept.
  2. Setup Inequalities: Use the specific values given in the graph (e.g., y-int <3, x-int >5) to form inequalities.
  3. Solve Carefully: Isolate a and b, paying close attention to the direction of inequality signs.
  4. Compare: Use the derived ranges to validate the statements I, II, and III.
