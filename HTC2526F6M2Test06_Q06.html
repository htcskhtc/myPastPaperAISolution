Question in Latex:
  1. Consider the parabola C:y=x2+4. C cuts the y-axis at the point P. Q is a moving point on C with h as its x-coordinate, where h>0. The vertical line which passes through Q cuts the x-axis at the point R. Denote the origin by O. Let A be the area of the quadrilateral OPQR.
    1. By expressing A in terms of h, find dAdh.
    2. Suppose that h=32et, where t (t>0) is the time measured in seconds. Someone claims that the rate of change of A has only one maximum value. Do you agree? Explain your answer. (7 marks)

Solution

Part (a): Find Area A and dAdh

First, identify the coordinates of the vertices of the quadrilateral OPQR.

  • C:y=x2+4
  • P is the y-intercept (x=0): P=(0,4).
  • Q lies on C with x-coordinate h: Q=(h,h2+4).
  • R lies on the x-axis directly below Q: R=(h,0).
  • O is the origin: O=(0,0).
Concept Note: Geometric Shape Since OP lies on the y-axis (vertical) and RQ is a vertical line (x=h), OP is parallel to RQ. Therefore, OPQR is a trapezium (or trapezoid). The height of the trapezium is the horizontal distance between the parallel sides, which is h.

Area of trapezium A=12(sum of parallel sides)height

A=12(OP+RQ)OR A=12(4+(h2+4))h A=12(h2+8)h A=12h3+4h

Now, differentiate A with respect to h:

dAdh=ddh(12h3+4h) dAdh=32h2+4
Part (b): Rate of Change of A

We are given h=32et for t>0. We need to analyze the rate of change of A. Let the rate of change of A be dAdt.

Common Trap: Variable Confusion The question asks about the "rate of change of A". In calculus problems involving time t, this implicitly means dAdt, not dAdh. You must use the Chain Rule.

Using the Chain Rule:

dAdt=dAdhdhdt

First, find dhdt:

h=32et dhdt=02(et)=2et

Substitute dAdh from Part (a) and dhdt into the Chain Rule formula:

dAdt=(32h2+4)(2et) dAdt=(3h2+8)et

To analyze the maximum value, it is easier to express dAdt entirely in terms of t. Substitute h=32et:
