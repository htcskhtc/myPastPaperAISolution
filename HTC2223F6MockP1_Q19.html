Question 19
Let f(x)=2x216kx8x+32k2+31k+18, where k is a positive constant. On the same rectangular coordinate system, denote the vertex of the graph of y=f(x) and the vertex of the graph of y=f(2x22)14 by Q and R respectively.
  1. Using the method of completing the square, express the coordinates of Q in terms of k. (2 marks)
  2. Write down the coordinates of R in terms of k. (1 mark)
  3. The coordinates of S are (4k2,k4). It is given that Q, R and S are three distinct points. Denote the circumcentre of QRS by G.
    1. Express the equation of the perpendicular bisector of QS in terms of k.
    2. Express the coordinates of G in terms of k.
    3. Denote the circle passing through Q, R and S by C. Let A be a point outside C such that AQ and AS are the tangents to C at Q and S respectively. Is it possible that AQGS is a square? Explain your answer.
    (9 marks)

Concept: To complete the square for x2bx, we add and subtract (b2)2. Here, b=8k+4, so b2=4k+2.
f(x)=2[x22(4k+2)x+(4k+2)2(4k+2)2]+32k2+31k+18=2[(x(4k+2))2(4k+2)2]+32k2+31k+18=2(x(4k+2))22(16k2+16k+4)+32k2+31k+18=2(x(4k+2))232k232k8+32k2+31k+18=2(x(4k+2))2k+10 The vertex form is y=a(xh)2+kvertex. Key Notes for this Question
  • Completing the Square: To convert ax2+bx+c to vertex form, factor out a first, then add/subtract (b/2a)2.
  • Function Transformation: y=f(2x) affects the x-coordinate (compression), while the outer constant affects the y-coordinate.
  • Circumcentre: The intersection of perpendicular bisectors. If two points share a coordinate (horizontal/vertical line), the perp. bisector is very easy to find (vertical/horizontal).
  • Geometry Logic: For "Is it possible" questions, assume the condition is true, solve for the variable, and check if the result is valid (e.g., k>0).
Notes / Concepts
  • Vertex Form: y=a(xh)2+kV(h,k)
  • Square & Tangents: A kite formed by two tangents and two radii is a square if the angle at the center is 90 (or radius = tangent length).
  • Circumcentre (G): Equidistant from vertices. Intersection of bisectors.
Strategy
  • Coordinate Geometry: When given variables (k), keep them in the calculation until the final step.
  • Simplification: Look for horizontal/vertical lines (like RS here) to simplify equations of lines.
  • Existence Checks: Set up the equation for the desired shape (Square), solve for k, and verify constraints (k>0, distinct points).
