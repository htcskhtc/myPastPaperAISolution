Solution

Part (a): Equation of Locus

Given that BP=AB, the distance from the point P to the fixed point B is constant (equal to length AB).
Therefore, the locus is a circle with center B(1,2) and radius r=AB.

Concept Note: Locus of Points The condition BP=constant defines a circle. The equation of a circle with center (h,k) and radius r is (xh)2+(yk)2=r2.

First, calculate the square of the radius, AB2: AB2=[5(1)]2+(102)2 AB2=(6)2+(8)2 AB2=36+64=100 The equation of is: (x(1))2+(y2)2=100 (x+1)2+(y2)2=100

Part (b)(iii): Max Area of AHK

The area of AHK=12baseheight.
We treat AH as the fixed base. To maximize the area, we must maximize the perpendicular distance (height) from point K (on the circle ) to the line passing through A and H.

Figure: Maximum area occurs when K is furthest from chord AH
Common Trap Students often try to find a specific coordinate for K or assume AHK is isosceles. The rigorous method is to calculate the maximum geometric distance from the circle to the line AH.
Max Height = Distance from Center to Line + Radius

Step 1: Find the equation of line AH.
Slope of AH=1025(11)=816=12.
Equation of AH: y2=12(x(11)) 2y4=x+11 x2y+15=0

Step 2: Find the distance from center B(1,2) to line AH (Alternative: Area Method).
Notice that H(11,2) and B(1,2) share the same y-coordinate (y=2).
Distance HB=1(11)=10.
The perpendicular height of A(5,10) from the line HB is 102=8.

Area of ABH=12base HBheight=12108=40.

Let d be the perpendicular distance from B to line AH. Using AH (found in b(ii) as 85) as the base: Area=12AHd 40=12(85)d 80=85d d=105=25

Step 3: Calculate Max Area.
The maximum height of the triangle corresponds to the point on the circle furthest from the chord. hmax=d+radius=25+10 Max Area =12AHhmax =12(85)(25+10) =45(25+10) =4(2)(5)+405 =40+405

Step 4: Check the Claim.
Is 40+405130?
Check: 40540(2.236)=89.44
Max Area 40+89.44=129.44
Since 129.44<130, the area cannot exceed 130.

The claim is correct.

Key Notes for Q12:
  • Locus Definition: BP=AB immediately implies a circle centered at B with radius AB.
  • Optimization in Geometry: For a fixed base, Area is max when height is max. On a circle, the point furthest from a chord is at a distance of dcenter+r.

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes Formulas to Memorize:
  • Circle Equation: (xh)2+(yk)2=r2
  • Distance Formula: (x2x1)2+(y2y1)2
  • Perpendicular distance from point (x1,y1) to line Ax+By+C=0: |Ax1+By1+C|A2+B2
Strategy Optimization Problems involving Circles:
  • When asked to maximize the area of a triangle with two vertices fixed and one on a circle, identify the fixed base.
  • Maximize the height by calculating the distance from the circle's center to the base line (d).
  • The maximum height is always d+r (and minimum height is |dr|).
