Question 1

  1. Let (1+mx)(1+5x)n=k=0n+1akxk, where m is a constant and n is a positive integer. If a1=27 and a2=285, find m and n. (4 marks)

Solution

(1+mx)(1+5x)n=k=0n+1akxk. Using the binomial theorem, (1+5x)n=r=0n(nr)(5x)r=1+5nx+(n2)25x2+=1+5nx+25n(n1)2x2+
Concept Note: General Term When expanding (1+x)n, remember that the coefficient of xr is (nr). In this case, replace x with 5x, so the term is (nr)(5x)r.
Hence (1+mx)(1+5x)n=(1+5x)n+mx(1+5x)n. Coefficient of x: (1)a1=5n+m=27m=275n. Coefficient of x2: The x2-term comes from:
  • the x2-term of (1+5x)n, which is 25n(n1)2;
  • mx times the x-term of (1+5x)n, which is m(5n).
Common Trap: Coefficient Mixing When multiplying two polynomials, the coefficient of xk often comes from multiple pairings. Here, x2 is formed by (Constant x2) AND (xx). Students often forget the second part (mx5nx).
So (2)a2=25n(n1)2+5mn=285. Substitute m=275n from (1) into (2): 25n(n1)2+5n(275n)=285. 25(n2n)2+135n25n2=285. Exam Strategy Notes

AI-Generated Strategy (M2 Syllabus):

1. Comparing Coefficients: In identity problems involving expansions, equating coefficients of like powers (x1,x2,) is the standard method. Always identify how many equations you need based on the number of unknowns (here, m and n require 2 equations).

2. Integer Constraints: In DSE math, n in binomial expansions is typically a positive integer. If you solve a quadratic equation for n and get a non-integer, reject it. If both are non-integers, check your algebraic simplification immediately.

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes
  • Binomial Theorem: (a+b)n=r=0n(nr)anrbr.
  • Combination Formula: (nr)=n!r!(nr)!. Specifically, (n1)=n and (n2)=n(n1)2.
  • Polynomial Identity: If two polynomials are identical, their corresponding coefficients are equal.
Strategy
  1. Expand Selectively: Do not expand the whole bracket. Only write down terms up to the power required (in this case, up to x2).
  2. Systematic Pairing: When multiplying (1+mx)(...), systematically pair terms to form the target power (e.g., 1x2 and xx).
  3. Substitution: Form simultaneous equations and use substitution (sub m into equation with n) to solve.
Required Skill
  • Manipulating binomial coefficients.
  • Solving simultaneous equations involving a quadratic.
  • Rejecting invalid roots based on domain constraints (nZ+).
