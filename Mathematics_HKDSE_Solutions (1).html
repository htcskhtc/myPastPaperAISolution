  1. Simplify (x4y5)3xy2 and express your answer with positive indices. (3 marks)
Solution
(x4y5)3xy2 Step 1: Power of a product
Concept Note: Apply the law (ab)n=anbn. The exponent outside the bracket applies to every factor inside.
(x4y5)3=(x4)3(y5)3 Step 2: Power of a power
Concept Note: Apply the law (am)n=amn. Multiply the indices, do not add them.
Common Trap: Be careful with signs! 53=15.
(x4)3=x12,(y5)3=y15 So the numerator becomes: x12y15 Step 3: Divide by xy2
Concept Note: Apply the law aman=amn. Remember that the single x in the denominator has an implicit index of 1 (i.e., x1).
Common Trap: For the y term, calculation is 152, which equals 17. A common mistake is to calculate 152=13.
x12y15xy2=(x12x)(y15y2)=x121y152=x11y17 Step 4: Write with positive indices
Concept Note: The question strictly requires "positive indices". Use the law an=1an to move the negative power to the denominator.
x11y17=x11y17 \boxed{\frac{x^{11}}{y^{17}}}
