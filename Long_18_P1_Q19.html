Question 19

Figure 3 shows a regular tetrahedron VABC of side a cm. M and N are the mid-points of VB and VC respectively.

  1. Find MAN. (4 marks)
  2. It is given that X and Y are points on VB and VC respectively such that VX=VY, VX<12VB and VY<12VC. Tommy claims that XAY is smaller than MAN. Do you agree? Explain your answer. (4 marks)

Solution

Part (a): Find MAN

Figure: MAN highlighted in blue.

Step 1: Identify Side Lengths

Let the side length of the regular tetrahedron be a. Since all faces are equilateral triangles, all edges are length a and all face angles are 60.

1. Find length MN:
Consider VBC. M and N are mid-points of VB and VC.
By the Mid-point Theorem:

MN=12BC=12a

2. Find length AM:
Consider VAB. It is an equilateral triangle with side a.
M is the mid-point of VB, so AMVB (Median is also altitude in equilateral ).
Using Pythagoras' theorem or trigonometry (AM=ABsin60):

AM=asin60=32a

3. Find length AN:
Similarly, in equilateral VAC, N is the mid-point of VC.

AN=asin60=32a
Concept Note: Heights in Equilateral Triangles In a regular tetrahedron, the faces are identical equilateral triangles. The median (AM) drawn from a vertex to the midpoint of the opposite side is also the height.
Height h=sidesin(60)=side32.
Step 2: Apply Cosine Rule

Now consider MAN with sides AM=32a, AN=32a, and MN=a2.
By the Cosine Rule:

cosMAN=AM2+AN2MN22(AM)(AN) cosMAN=(32a)2+(32a)2(12a)22(32a)(32a)

Simplify terms (note that a2 cancels out):

cosMAN=34+34142(34) cosMAN=5464=56 MAN=cos1(56)33.5573

Answer: MAN33.6 (correct to 3 sig. fig.)

Part (b): Tommy's Claim

Figure: Points X,Y are closer to V than M,N.

Step 1: Express Side Lengths in terms of x

Let VX=VY=x. Given that x<a2.
We need to compare XAY with MAN. Since the cosine function is decreasing for angles between 0 and 180, if cosXAY>cosMAN, then XAY<MAN.

1. Find length XY:
VXY is similar to VBC (or use Cosine rule with 60). Actually, since face VBC is equilateral, VXY is also equilateral with side x.

XY=x

2. Find lengths AX and AY:
Consider VAX. VA=a, VX=x, and AVX=60 (face angle).
Using Cosine Rule:

AX2=VA2+VX22(VA)(VX)cos60 AX2=a2+x22ax(12)=a2ax+x2

Similarly, for VAY:

AY2=a2ax+x2

So, AX=AY=a2ax+x2.

Step 2: Set up Cosine Rule for XAY

In XAY:

cosXAY=AX2+AY2XY22(AX)(AY)

Substitute AX2=AY2=a2ax+x2 and XY2=x2:

cosXAY=(a2ax+x2)+(a2ax+x2)x22(a2ax+x2) cosXAY=2a22ax+x22(a2ax+x2)
Step 3: Compare with cosMAN

We know from part (a) that cosMAN=56.
We want to verify if XAY<MAN. This is equivalent to checking if:

cosXAY>56 2a22ax+x22(a2ax+x2)>56
Common Trap: Inequality Direction Remember that cos decreases as increases (for acute angles).
So, "Angle is smaller" means "Cosine value is larger".

Let's simplify the inequality. Multiply both sides by 6(a2ax+x2) (which is positive since it represents a squared length):

3(2a22ax+x2)>5(a2ax+x2) 6a26ax+3x2>5a25ax+5x2

Rearrange terms to one side:

a2ax2x2>0

Factorize the quadratic expression in terms of a and x:

(a+x)(a2x)>0

Analyze the factors:

  • Since a and x are lengths, a>0 and x>0. Therefore, (a+x) is always positive.
  • The question states VX<12VB, which means x<12a.
  • Multiplying by 2, we get 2x<a, or a2x>0.

Since both factors (a+x) and (a2x) are positive, their product is positive.

(a+x)(a2x)>0 is TRUE.

Therefore, the inequality cosXAY>56 holds true.

Conclusion:
Since cosXAY>cosMAN, it implies XAY<MAN.
Yes, I agree with Tommy's claim.

Notes & Strategies

Key Takeaways from this Question:

  • Regular Tetrahedron Properties: All faces are equilateral triangles. Median, Altitude, and Angle Bisector coincide in equilateral triangles.
  • Mid-point Theorem: The segment connecting midpoints of two sides of a triangle is parallel to the third side and half the length.
  • Cosine Rule Comparison: To compare angles without calculating the exact value, compare their cosine values. Remember the inverse relationship for acute angles.
  • Algebraic Verification: When asked to prove an inequality involving geometry, convert geometric conditions (like x<a/2) into algebraic inequalities.
Notes
  • Formula for height of equilateral triangle: h=32a.
  • Cosine Rule: c2=a2+b22abcosC.
  • cos is a decreasing function for 0<<180.
Strategy
  • Part (a): Isolate the relevant cross-section triangle (MAN). Find all three sides using basic trigonometry and Mid-point theorem. Apply Cosine Rule.
  • Part (b): Define a variable (x). Express sides of the new triangle in terms of x and a. Set up an inequality comparing the new Cosine value to the old one. Solve the resulting quadratic inequality using the given range (x<a/2).
Required Skill
  • 3D Geometry visualization.
  • Application of Sine/Cosine Rules.
  • Solving Quadratic Inequalities.
