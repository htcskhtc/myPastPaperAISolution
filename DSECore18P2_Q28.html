Q28 (Ref: DSE18PII_Q28)
  1. Two numbers are randomly drawn at the same time from seven cards numbered 1,1,1,2,2,3 and 4 respectively. Find the probability that the sum of the numbers drawn is 5.
    1. 521
    2. 542
    3. 549
    4. 1049
Solution
Key Concept: Combinations vs Permutations The phrase "drawn at the same time" implies that the order does not matter. Therefore, we use Combinations (nCr or Crn) instead of Permutations (Prn). Also, unlike "drawing with replacement", once a card is picked, it cannot be picked again for the second slot.
There are seven cards with numbers 1,1,1,2,2,3,4. Two cards are drawn at the same time (i.e. without replacement and the order does not matter).

Step 1: Total number of possible pairs The total number of ways to choose 2 cards from 7 is Total number of outcomes=7C2=7621=21.
Common Trap: Identical Digits Students often mistakenly think that because there are three '1's, they count as just one type of item. In probability, physical objects are distinct. Each card is a separate entity. There are 7 total cards, so n=7, regardless of the numbers printed on them.


Step 2: Favourable outcomes (sum = 5) We want the sum of the two numbers drawn to be 5. The possible pairs of values that give sum 5 are 1+4=5and2+3=5. Case 1: Pair (1,4) There are three 1's and one 4. The number of ways to choose one 1 and one 4 is 3C11C1=31=3. Case 2: Pair (2,3) There are two 2's and one 3. The number of ways to choose one 2 and one 3 is 2C11C1=21=2. Hence, the total number of favourable outcomes is 3+2=5.

Step 3: Probability Therefore, the required probability is P(sum=5)=number of favourable outcomestotal number of outcomes=521. So, the probability that the sum of the numbers drawn is 5 is 521.

This keyword is your trigger to use the Combination formula (nCr). It simplifies the math compared to calculating "First card is 1, Second is 4" OR "First is 4, Second is 1".

When you split the problem into "Case 1" (getting 1 & 4) and "Case 2" (getting 2 & 3), you add the results (3+2). Do not multiply them.

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes
  • Formula: nCr=n!r!(nr)! is used for selecting items where order implies no difference.
  • Distinction: Even if cards have the same face value (like the three '1's), they are treated as distinct items for the total count (n=7).
Strategy
  1. Identify Total Space: Calculate 7C2 immediately to set the denominator.
  2. List Target Sums: Systematically list pairs that sum to 5: (1,4) and (2,3). (1,2,2) is impossible as we only draw 2 cards.
  3. Calculate Cases: Calculate ways to form each pair using available counts of each digit.
  4. Sum & Divide: Add favourable cases and divide by total.
