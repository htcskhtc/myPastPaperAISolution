Question 19

Let f(x)=x212kx14x+36k2+89k+53, where k is a positive constant. On the same rectangular coordinate system, denote the vertex of the graph of y=f(x) and the vertex of the graph of y=f(14x) by Q and R respectively.

  1. Using the method of completing the square, express, in terms of k, the coordinates of Q. (2 marks)
  2. Write down, in terms of k, the coordinates of R. (1 mark)
  3. The coordinates of the point S are (7,43k). Denote the inscribed circle of QRS by C.
    1. Express, in terms of k, the equation of the straight line which passes through Q and S.
    2. Express, in terms of k, the equation of C.
    3. Suppose that QS is the tangent to C at the point T. Let U be the centre of C. It is given that the coordinates of the point V are (29,14). Is it possible that STUV is a rectangle? Explain your answer. (9 marks)

Solution

Part (a)

f(x)=x212kx14x+36k2+89k+53=x22(6k+7)x+36k2+89k+53=[x22(6k+7)x+(6k+7)2](6k+7)2+36k2+89k+53=(x(6k+7))2(36k2+84k+49)+36k2+89k+53=(x(6k+7))2+5k+4 The coordinates of the vertex Q are (6k+7,5k+4).

Concept Note: For a quadratic function in vertex form y=a(xh)2+k, the vertex is at (h,k).
Part (b)

The graph of y=f(14x) is a reflection of y=f(x) about the line x=7 (since the midpoint of x and 14x is 7).
Alternatively, consider the transformation: The vertex of y=f(t) occurs at t=6k+7.
Here, let t=14x. Then 14x=6k+7x=14(6k+7)=76k.
The y-coordinate remains unchanged.
The coordinates of R are (76k,5k+4).

Common Trap: Students often confuse f(14x) with a horizontal shift. It involves a reflection because the coefficient of x inside the function is negative.
Part (c)(i)

Coordinates: Q(6k+7,5k+4) and S(7,43k).
Slope of QS: mQS=(5k+4)(43k)(6k+7)7=8k6k=43 Equation of line QS: y(43k)=43(x7)3(y4+3k)=4(x7)3y12+9k=4x284x3y169k=0

Part (c)(ii)

Q=(7+6k,5k+4) and R=(76k,5k+4).
Note that Q and R share the same y-coordinate, so QR is a horizontal line segment. The midpoint of QR is (7,5k+4).
Since the x-coordinate of S is also 7, QRS is an isosceles triangle symmetric about the line x=7.
The incenter U must lie on this axis of symmetry x=7.

Let r be the radius of the inscribed circle C.
Base QR=(7+6k)(76k)=12k.
Height of QRS=(5k+4)(43k)=8k (since k>0, S is below QR).
Area of QRS=1212k8k=48k2.

We need the side lengths. The distance from the midpoint (7,5k+4) to Q is 6k.
Side QS=(6k)2+(8k)2=36k2+64k2=100k2=10k.
By symmetry, RS=10k.
Semi-perimeter s=QR+QS+RS2=12k+10k+10k2=16k.

Using the formula Area =rs: r=Areas=48k216k=3k The center U lies on x=7. Since the triangle points downwards from the base QR, the incenter is below the base and above S. However, it is easier to calculate the vertical distance from S.
Wait, let us verify the position. The distance from the line QR to the center U is r=3k.
The y-coordinate of QR is 5k+4.
Thus, y-coordinate of U=(5k+4)3k=2k+4.
(Check distance from S(7,43k): (2k+4)(43k)=5k. This is valid since the incenter divides the height in a specific ratio or simply by geometry).

Equation of circle C: (x7)2+(y(2k+4))2=(3k)2 (x7)2+(y2k4)2=9k2

Strategy: For isosceles triangles, the incenter always lies on the axis of symmetry. The formula r=Areas is the most efficient way to find the inradius.
Part (c)(iii)

It is given that QS is tangent to C at T. Thus STU=90 (radius tangent).
For STUV to be a rectangle, the remaining interior angles must be 90. Specifically, we require VST=90 and TUV=90.

Condition 1: VST=90
This implies that the line segment SV is perpendicular to ST (which is part of the line QS).
Slope of QS, mQS=43.
Slope of SV: mSV=14(43k)297=3k1836=(6k)123 (simplify later) mSV=3k1836=k612 For perpendicularity: mQSmSV=1 43k612=1 k69=1k6=9k=15
Condition 2: Parallel Sides (STUV)
For STUV to be a rectangle, opposite sides must be parallel. Thus UVST (line QS).
Slope of UV: U=(7,2k+4),V=(29,14) mUV=14(2k+4)297=2k1836=2(k+9)36=k+918 For parallelism (mUV=mQS): k+918=43 k+9=1843=24 k=15
Since both geometric conditions for a rectangle (right angle at S and parallel opposite sides) are satisfied by the same positive value k=15, it is geometrically possible.

Conclusion: Yes, it is possible when k=15.

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes
  • Vertex Form: y=a(xh)2+k has vertex (h,k).
  • Reflection: y=f(2ax) reflects y=f(x) across x=a.
  • Incenter: The center of the inscribed circle. For an isosceles triangle, it lies on the axis of symmetry.
  • Radius of Incircle: r=Areasemi-perimeter.
Strategy
  • Completing the Square: Expand, regroup terms involving x, and form the perfect square.
  • Coordinate Geometry: When proving a shape is a rectangle, check if adjacent sides are perpendicular (slope product = -1) or if diagonals bisect each other and are equal length.
  • Consistency Check: If multiple conditions must be met (e.g., perpendicularity and parallelism), solve for the unknown variable (k) in one condition and verify it satisfies the others.
Required Skill
  • Manipulation of algebraic expressions with constants (k).
  • Understanding function transformations (f(14x)).
  • Finding the incenter of a triangle.
  • Using slopes to determine geometric properties (tangents, rectangles).
