Question Code: DSE25PII_Q17

In the figure, ABCD is a parallelogram. Let E, F and G be points lying on AB, AD and CD respectively such that BE=3AE, 2DF=3AF and DG=2CG. It is given that BC produced and EG produced meet at the point H. If the area of CGH is 16 cm2, then the area of the quadrilateral DFEG is

  1. 46 cm2.
  2. 49 cm2.
  3. 105 cm2.
  4. 115 cm2.

Solution

Step 1: Analyze the Given Ratios

First, we interpret the given conditions to find the ratios of the lengths of the segments relative to the parallelogram's sides. Let AB=CD=x and AD=BC=y.

  • BE=3AEAE:AB=1:4. Thus, AE=14x and BE=34x.
  • 2DF=3AFAF:DF=2:3AF:AD=2:5. Thus, AF=25y and DF=35y.
  • DG=2CGCG:CD=1:3. Thus, CG=13x and DG=23x.

Step 2: Use Similar Triangles to Find Area Relationships

Since ABCD is a parallelogram, ABDC. Therefore, EBGC. Consider HCG and HBE. Since GCEB, HCGHBE.

The ratio of similarity is:

CGBE=13x34x=1343=49

Now, apply the area ratio formula:

Area(HCG)Area(HBE)=(49)2=1681
Concept Note: Area of Similar Figures Remember that if two figures are similar with a side ratio (linear scale factor) of l1l2=k, their area ratio is k2. Don't forget to square the ratio!

Step 3: Calculate the Area of HBE and Trapezium EBCG

Given Area(HCG)=16 cm2.

16Area(HBE)=1681Area(HBE)=81 cm2

The area of the trapezium EBCG is the difference:

Area(EBCG)=Area(HBE)Area(HCG)=8116=65 cm2

Step 4: Find the Total Area of Parallelogram ABCD

To find the area of the quadrilateral DFEG, a good strategy is the "Subtraction Method":

Area(DFEG)=Area(ABCD)Area(EBCG)Area(AEF)

We need to find the total area of the parallelogram ABCD. Let's use the height method. Let h be the height of the parallelogram (distance between AB and CD). Let h1 be the height of HCG (distance from H to CG). Let h2 be the height of HBE (distance from H to BE).

From similarity HCGHBE, the ratio of heights is also 4:9.

h1h2=49h1=49h2

Also, clearly h2=h1+h.

h1=49(h1+h)9h1=4h1+4h5h1=4hh1=0.8h
Common Trap: Height Confusion It is easy to assume the height of the triangle relates directly to the height of the parallelogram without calculation. Always set up the equation htotal=hsmall+hpara to find the correct relationship (h1=0.8h here).

We know Area(HCG)=12CGh1=16. Substitute CG=13AB and h1=0.8h:

12(13AB)(0.8h)=16 0.86(ABh)=16

Since ABh=Area(ABCD):

Area(ABCD)=1660.8=167.5=120 cm2

Step 5: Calculate Area of AEF and Solve

Area(AEF)=12AEAFsinA Area(ABCD)=ABADsinA=120

Substituting ratios AE=14AB and AF=25AD:

Area(AEF)=12(14AB)(25AD)sinA Area(AEF)=(121425)(ABADsinA) Area(AEF)=120120=6 cm2

Finally, calculate Area(DFEG):

Area(DFEG)=120656=49 cm2

The correct option is B.

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes
  • Area Ratio: For similar triangles with side ratio l1:l2, the area ratio is l12:l22.
  • Area Formulas:
    Parallelogram = Base Height (bh) or absin.
    Triangle = 12 Base Height or 12absin.
  • Similarity Condition: Parallel lines (EBGC) imply alternate angles are equal, creating similar triangles.
Strategy
  • Subtraction Method: When finding the area of an irregular polygon (like DFEG), calculate the total area of the enclosing shape (ABCD) and subtract the easier "corner" shapes (AEF, EBCG).
  • Height Ratio: Use the similar triangle ratio to express the height of the extension (HCG) in terms of the parallelogram's height.
  • Variable Substitution: Even without actual lengths, assume AB=x and AD=y (or use Area terms directly) to track ratios.
Required Skill
  • Properties of Parallelograms (parallel sides).
  • Properties of Similar Triangles (ratio of sides and areas).
  • Algebraic manipulation of ratios.
