Question:
  1. The solution of 4y+1<5y38y9 is
    1. y>4.
    2. y2.
    3. y2.
    4. y>4.

Solution:

We solve the compound inequality 4y+1<5y38y9.
Concept Note: A double inequality in the form A<BC is handled by splitting it into two separate inequalities: A<B AND BC. The final solution must satisfy both conditions (intersection).
This is equivalent to the system {4y+1<5y3,5y38y9. First inequality: 4y+1<5y3,1<y3(subtract 4y from both sides),4<y(add 3 to both sides),y>4. Second inequality: 5y38y9,33y9(subtract 5y from both sides),63y(add 9 to both sides),2y(divide both sides by 3),y2. Hence, the solution must satisfy both y>4andy2. The common part is y>4.
Common Trap: When combining ranges, visualize them on a number line. If a number is greater than 4, it is automatically greater than or equal to 2. Therefore, the intersection is just y>4. Do not confuse "AND" (overlap) with "OR" (union).
Therefore, the solution of 4y+1<5y38y9 is y>4, which corresponds to option (D).
Exam Strategy & Skills

Topic: Linear Inequalities in One Unknown (Compound)

Key Logic:

  • The notation a<bc is shorthand for the conjunction (AND) of two inequalities.
  • Always solve the two parts separately: (1) Left part vs Middle part, and (2) Middle part vs Right part.
  • Final Answer = Intersection of the solution sets of (1) and (2).

Visualization Skill: Use a number line to draw the two ranges. The answer is where the lines overlap.

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes
  • Compound Inequality Rule: A<BCA<B AND BC.
  • Keywords: "AND" means Intersection (Overlap). "OR" means Union (Combine everything).
  • Double Check: Ensure the direction of inequality signs is reversed if you divide/multiply by a negative number (though not applied in this specific question, it is a crucial general rule).
Strategy
  1. Split: Break the double inequality into two separate linear inequalities.
  2. Solve: Solve for y in each inequality independently.
  3. Visualize: Sketch a number line. Mark the boundary points (4 and 2). Draw arrows indicating the direction.
  4. Intersect: Identify the region covered by BOTH arrows.
