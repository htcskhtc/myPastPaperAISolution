Question 41
  1. Let O be the origin. The coordinates of the points A and B are (a,0) and (0,b) respectively, where a and b are positive numbers. If the in-centre of OAB lies on the straight line 2x+y=b, then a:b=
    1. 3:4.
    2. 4:3.
    3. 8:15.
    4. 15:8.
Solution

Step 1: Understand the Geometry
OAB is a right-angled triangle with AOB=90 (since A is on the x-axis and B is on the y-axis).

The lengths of the sides are:

  • OA=a
  • OB=b
  • AB=a2+b2 (Pythagoras' Theorem)

Concept Note: In-centre of a Right Triangle For a right-angled triangle located at the origin with positive leg lengths a and b, the in-centre I forms a square with the perpendicular radii to the axes. Therefore, the coordinates of the in-centre are (r,r), where r is the in-radius.

Step 2: Find the in-radius r
For a right-angled triangle with legs a,b and hypotenuse c, the in-radius r is given by the formula: r=a+bc2 Substituting c=a2+b2: r=a+ba2+b22 Thus, the coordinates of the in-centre are: (a+ba2+b22,a+ba2+b22)

Step 3: Substitute into the line equation
We are given that the in-centre lies on the line 2x+y=b.
Let x=r and y=r. 2(r)+(r)=b 3r=b

Step 4: Solve for the ratio a:b
Substitute the expression for r into 3r=b: 3(a+ba2+b22)=b Multiply both sides by 2: 3(a+ba2+b2)=2b 3a+3b3a2+b2=2b Isolate the square root term: 3a+b=3a2+b2 Square both sides to eliminate the root: (3a+b)2=(3a2+b2)2 9a2+6ab+b2=9(a2+b2) 9a2+6ab+b2=9a2+9b2 Subtract 9a2 from both sides: 6ab+b2=9b2 6ab=8b2 Since b is a positive number, we can divide both sides by 2b2 (or divide by b first then rearrange): 6a2b=8b2b 3(ab)=4 ab=43 a:b=4:3

The correct answer is (B).
