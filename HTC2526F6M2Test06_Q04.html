Question 4
  1. (a) Using integration by substitution, prove that x2(4+x2)2dx=14tan1(x2)x2(4+x2)+constant.
  2. (b) Consider the curve C:y=x4+x2. Let R be the region bounded by C, the straight line x=2 and the x-axis. Find the volume of the solid of revolution generated by revolving R about the x-axis. (8 marks)

Notes on this Question

1. Trig Substitution Standard Forms:
- For a2x2, let x=asin.
- For a2+x2 or a2+x2, let x=atan.
- For x2a2, let x=asec.

2. Double Angle Formula Integration:
Direct integration of sin2x or cos2x is difficult. Always convert using: sin2=1cos22 and cos2=1+cos22.

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes
  • Identity to Memorize: sin2=12(1cos2).
  • Differentiation of Inverse Tangent: ddx(tan1x)=11+x2 (useful for checking).
  • Volume Formula: Rotation about x-axis: V=y2dx.
Strategy
  • Step 1 (Substitution): Simplify the algebraic fraction by converting it to trigonometry.
  • Step 2 (Triangle Method): Draw the reference triangle immediately after finding the integral in to ensure accurate conversion back to x.
  • Step 3 (Linkage): Recognize that Part (b) is usually a direct application of Part (a). Look for y2 matching the integrand in (a).
