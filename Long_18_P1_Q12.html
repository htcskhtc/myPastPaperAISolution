Question 12

In Figure 1, ABCD is a trapezium with AD//BC. E is a point lying on CD such that AE and BE are the angle bisectors of BAD and ABC respectively.

(a) Is ABE a right-angled triangle? Explain your answer. (2 marks)

(b) Let P be a point on AB such that ADEAPE.

  • (i) Name a triangle which is congruent to BCE. Give the proof.
  • (ii) If AE=12 cm and BE=5 cm, find the length of AD+BC. (5 marks)

Solution

Part (a)

Since AD//BC,

DAB+CBA=180 (int. s, AD//BC)

Concept Note: Remember that for parallel lines, the interior angles on the same side of the transversal sum to 180.

Since AE and BE are angle bisectors of BAD and ABC respectively,

EAB=12DAB and EBA=12CBA.

In ABE,

EAB+EBA=12(DAB+CBA)

=12(180)=90

Therefore, AEB=180(EAB+EBA) ( sum of )

AEB=18090=90

Conclusion: Yes, ABE is a right-angled triangle.

Part (b)(i)

Target: Prove BCEBPE.

Common Trap: Do not assume congruence by appearance (e.g., SAS) without verifying lengths or angles. Since we don't have side lengths for BC or BP yet, look for Angle properties first.

Given ADEAPE,

ADE=APE (corr. s, s)

Since ABCD is a trapezium (AD//BC),

ADC+BCD=180 (int. s, AD//BC)

Also, APE+BPE=180 (adj. s on st. line)

Since ADE=ADC (given points are collinear),

BPE=180APE=180ADE=BCD.

In BCE and BPE:

  1. BCE=BPE (proved)
  2. CBE=PBE (given BE bisects ABC)
  3. BE=BE (common side)

BCEBPE (AAS)

Part (b)(ii)

We need to find AD+BC.

From (b)(i), BCEBPE,

BC=BP (corr. sides, s)

From given ADEAPE,

AD=AP (corr. sides, s)

Strategy: Express the sum AD+BC in terms of segments on the single line AB.

AD+BC=AP+BP=AB

In right-angled ABE (from part a), using Pythagoras' theorem:

AB2=AE2+BE2

AB=122+52

AB=144+25=169=13 cm

AD+BC=13 cm.

Note in Latex (AI Generated)
  • Bisector Property: When two interior angles between parallel lines are bisected, the bisectors meet at 90. This is a standard result worth memorizing for speed.
  • Congruence Proofs: When sides are unknown, rely on angle chasing. Linking interior angles of parallel lines (D+C=180) with angles on a straight line (APE+BPE=180) is a classic technique to transfer angles from one location to another.
  • Construction Hint: The point P effectively "unfolds" the trapezium sides onto the base AB.

Strategy
  • Part (a): Combine parallel line angle properties with bisector definitions to find the sum of half-angles.
  • Part (b)(i): Use the relationship between interior angles and angles on a straight line to prove angle equality (C=BPE).
