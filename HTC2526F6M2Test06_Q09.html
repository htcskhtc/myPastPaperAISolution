HKDSE M2 Vector Solution

Question

The position vectors of the points A, B and C are i2j+3k, 2i+5j+2k and 2ik respectively. Denote the plane containing A, B and C by . Let E be the projection of the point D(2,6,2) on . It is given that EA produced meets BC at the point F.

  1. Find ABAC. (3 marks)
  2. Find ED and EA. (4 marks)
  3. If BF:FC=1:t, find t. (3 marks)
  4. Find the volume of the pyramid ABDF. (3 marks)

Step-by-Step Solution

(a) Finding the Cross Product

First, we determine the coordinates of the points from their position vectors:

  • A=(1,2,3)
  • B=(2,5,2)
  • C=(2,0,1)

Calculate the vectors AB and AC:

AB=ba=(21)i+(5(2))j+(23)k=3i+7jk AC=ca=(21)i+(0(2))j+(13)k=i+2j4k

Now, calculate the cross product ABAC:

ABAC=|ijk371124|=i((7)(4)(1)(2))j((3)(4)(1)(1))+k((3)(2)(7)(1))=i(28+2)j(12+1)+k(67)=26i13j13k
Concept Note: Normal Vector The cross product ABAC produces a vector that is perpendicular (normal) to the plane containing A, B, and C. This normal vector is crucial for finding equations of planes or distances from points to planes.
Simplification Tip: While the answer is 26i13j13k, note that this is equal to 13(2i+j+k). Using the simpler direction vector (2,1,1) often makes subsequent calculations easier.

(b) Finding ED and EA

1. Finding AD:

First, calculate the vector from A to D using coordinates A(1,2,3) and D(2,6,2):

AD=da=(21)i+(6(2))j+(23)k AD=3i4jk

2. Finding ED (Projection Method):

From Part (a), the normal vector to the plane is n=26i13j13k. For simpler calculation, we use the parallel vector n=2i+j+k.

Since E is the projection of D on , the vector ED is the orthogonal projection of AD onto the normal vector n.

ED=projnAD=ADn|n|2n

Calculate the dot product ADn:

ADn=(3)(2)+(4)(1)+(1)(1)=641=11

Calculate the magnitude squared |n|2:

|n|2=22+12+12=6

Substitute back into the projection formula:

ED=116(2i+j+k) ED=113i116j116k

3. Finding EA:

Using vector addition triangle rule involving points A,E,D:

AD=AE+ED AE=ADED

Since we need EA (which is AE):

EA=EDAD EA=(113i116j116k)(3i4jk)

Group the components:

i:113(3)=113+93=23 j:116(4)=116+246=136 k:116(1)=116+66=56

Thus:

EA=23i+136j56k
Concept Note: Vector Projection Visualizing the geometry is key. E lies on the plane, D is outside. The vector AD connects a point on the plane (A) to D. Decomposing AD into components parallel and perpendicular to the plane yields AE and ED respectively.
Formula: ED=(ADn^)n^

(c) Finding t given BF:FC=1:t

1. Expressing AF in terms of t:

Let O be the origin. Since F lies on BC such that BF:FC=1:t, by the section formula:

OF=tOB+1OCt+1

Substitute OB=2i+5j+2k and OC=2ik:

OF=t(2i+5j+2k)+(2ik)t+1 OF=(2t+2)i+5tj+(2t1)kt+1

Now, find AF:

AF=OFOA=(2t+2)i+5tj+(2t1)kt+1(i2j+3k)

Combine terms component by component:

AF=2t+2(t+1)t+1i+5t(2)(t+1)t+1j+2t13(t+1)t+1k AF=3t+1t+1i+7t+2t+1j+t4t+1k

2. Setting up the vector equation:

It is given that EA produced meets BC at F. Thus, points E,A,F are collinear. We can write:

EA=AFfor some scalar 

From Part (b), we know EA=23i+136j56k. Substituting these vectors:

23i+136j56k=(3t+1t+1i+7t+2t+1j+t4t+1k)

3. Comparing coefficients to form equations:

Equating the i,j,k components gives three equations:

  1. 23=(3t+1t+1)
  2. 136=(7t+2t+1)
  3. 56=(t4t+1)

4. Solving for t:

To eliminate and solve for t, we can divide Equation (1) by Equation (2):

23136=(3t+1t+1)(7t+2t+1) 23613=3t+17t+2 413=3t+17t+2

Cross-multiply:

4(7t+2)=13(3t+1) 28t8=39t+13 11t=21 t=2111
Strategy Tip: Equation Selection Although we have three equations, we only needed two to find t. It is good practice to check your result with the third equation (Equation 3 / Equation 2) to ensure no calculation errors occurred.

(d) Volume of pyramid ABDF

1. Finding vector AF:

From Part (c), we have the expression for AF in terms of t:

AF=3t+1t+1i+7t+2t+1j+t4t+1k

Substitute t=2111:

t+1=2111+1=3211

Calculate the components:

  • i-component: 3(21/11)+132/11=(63+11)/1132/11=5232=138
  • j-component: 7(21/11)+232/11=(147+22)/1132/11=16932
  • k-component: (21/11)432/11=(2144)/1132/11=6532

Thus:

AF=138i+16932j6532k=1332(4i+13j5k)

2. Finding Area of ABF:

The area is given by 12|ABAF|.

We know AB=3i+7jk. Let's compute the cross product using the simplified direction vector for AF:

ABAF=1332[(3i+7jk)(4i+13j5k)]

Compute the determinant part:

|ijk3714135|=i(35(13))j(154)+k(39(28)) =22i11j11k=11(2i+j+k)

So:

ABAF=1332(11)(2i+j+k)=14332(2i+j+k)

Now, find the magnitude:

|ABAF|=1433222+12+12=143632

Area of ABF:

Area=12(143632)=143664

3. Finding the Volume of Pyramid ABDF:

The height of the pyramid is the perpendicular distance from D to the plane containing A,B,F (which is plane ). This distance is exactly |ED| found in Part (b).

From Part (b), ED=116(2i+j+k).

Height h=|ED|=11622+12+12=1166.

Finally, calculate the volume:

V=13Base AreaHeight V=131436641166 V=13143641166=14311643 V=1573192
Methodology Check This direct calculation confirms the result obtained by the ratio method. While calculation-heavy, finding the explicit area of the base and the specific height is a fundamental approach that works even if the geometry isn't "nice" (e.g., if you couldn't easily establish the ratio with a larger pyramid).

Consolidated Notes & Strategy

Notes
  • Cross Product: ab gives the normal vector to the plane containing a and b.
  • Vector Projection: projba=ab|b|2b. This is useful for finding perpendicular distances vectors.
  • Collinearity: Three points A,B,C are collinear if AB=kAC.
  • Volume of Pyramid: V=13(Base Area)(Height). The base area comes from the cross product magnitude, and the height comes from the projection vector magnitude.
Strategy
  • Part (b): Use the Projection Method. Calculate AD, project it onto the normal to find ED, then use vector subtraction for EA.
  • Part (c): Form a system of linear equations by comparing coefficients of i,j,k in the relation EA=AF. Solve by elimination.
  • Part (d): Use the Direct Method: Calculate exact vector AF, find Base Area via Cross Product magnitude, and multiply by Height (|ED|) and 13.
Required Skill
  • Calculating Cross Product (determinant method).
  • Vector Projection Formula.
  • Calculating Area of Triangle via Vector Magnitude.
